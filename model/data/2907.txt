name:
Classic puff pastries with broad beans, bacon, and pecorino
ingredients:
Crispy baked puff pastry: 12
Fava beans: 3.5oz(100 g)
Pancetta: 3.5oz(100 g)
Pecorino cheese: 1.8oz(50 g)- shavings
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 10 min
Makes: 12 pieces
Cost: Very low

To prepare the classic pastries with broad beans, bacon, and pecorino, start by shelling the fresh broad beans , then remove the skin that covers them by gently pressing with your fingers , collect them in a small bowl and season with oil , salt, and pepper to taste. As an alternative to fresh broad beans, you can use dried ones, soaking them for at least 12 hours and boiling them for a couple of hours or until they are cooked and softened.
Then cut the guanciale or sweet bacon into thin strips ; heat a non-stick pan with a drizzle of oil, then add the bacon strips . Sauté over high heat for a few minutes. Meanwhile, shave the pecorino into flakes using a grater or a small knife.
When the bacon is well browned, turn off the heat and assemble your pastries: place a teaspoon of broad beans , add one or two strips of bacon using kitchen tongs , and then some pecorino flakes. Serve your classic pastries with broad beans, bacon, and pecorino !
You can store the classic pastries with broad beans, bacon, and pecorino in the refrigerator for 1-2 days. Freezing is not recommended.
To add color to the classic pastries with broad beans, bacon, and pecorino, you can also add cherry tomatoes.
