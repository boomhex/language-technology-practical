name:
Salmon fillets en papillote
ingredients:
Salmon fillets: 1.75lbs(800 g)- (4 pieces)
Red onions: 2
Taggiasca olives: 24
Cherry tomatoes: 5
Parsley: 1tuft
Extra virgin olive oil: 4spoonfuls
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 35 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:
To prepare the salmon fillets en papillote, first remove any bones using kitchen tweezers . Peel and thinly slice the onions , then cut the cherry tomatoes into rounds .
Place 4 sheets of aluminum foil on the work surface and cover each with a sheet of parchment paper . Distribute the onions in the center of a sheet and season with a pinch of salt . Place a salmon fillet on top of the onions and season with salt and pepper .
Add 6 olives and distribute some sliced cherry tomatoes on top , lightly salt and drizzle with a little oil .
Close the packet by first lifting the edges of the parchment paper and folding the sides to obtain a well-sealed package , then do the same with the aluminum foil . Prepare the other 3 salmon fillets in the same way.
Transfer the packets to a baking sheet and cook in a preheated convection oven at 356°F for about 30-35 minutes. Once cooked, garnish with chopped parsley and serve your salmon fillets en papillote !
It is recommended to consume the salmon fillets en papillote immediately. If any leftovers remain, you can store them in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
You can replace the parsley with thyme or marjoram and add some pink peppercorns!
• How many ways can you cook salmon?
Many! Besides baking en papillote, you can opt for baked salmon, pan-fried, steamed, in the form of burgers, or tartare.
• What can I use instead of onions, olives, and cherry tomatoes to enrich the packet?
Seasonal diced vegetables like zucchini and eggplant, for example, or thinly sliced potatoes. Cooking times may vary.
• What is the difference between farmed salmon and wild salmon?
Wild salmon has leaner flesh and a more intense flavor.
• Is it true that salmon is one of the fish richest in Omega 3?
Yes: just 2 portions of salmon a week, about 5-7 oz each, are enough to meet the average weekly requirement of these essential fatty acids.
