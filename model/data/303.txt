name:
Gluten-free savory pie with spinach and tofu
ingredients:
Rice flour: ¾cup(120 g)
Water: 0.3cup(80 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: 0.7tbsp(10 g)
Fine salt: ⅓tsp(2 g)
Tofu: 7.1oz(200 g)
Spinach: 10cups(300 g)
Eggs: 1
Onions: 2.1oz(60 g)
Soy milk: 0.4cup(100 g)- unsweetened
Extra virgin olive oil: 1 ½tbsp(20 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Marjoram: to taste
Nutmeg: to taste

instructions:

To prepare the gluten-free savory pie with spinach and tofu, start with the base. In a bowl, put the rice flour and add the salt . Gradually pour in the water and knead . Finally, add the oil .
Once the oil is absorbed into the flour, move to your work surface and continue kneading until you obtain a compact dough . Place the dough on a sheet of parchment paper and, using a rolling pin , roll out a circular sheet with a diameter of 12 inches and about 1/8 inch thick.
Place the dough inside an 8-inch pan , then trim off the excess edges . Let the base rest in the refrigerator until ready to use. Now move on to the filling and start by slicing the onion .
In a pot, pour a drizzle of oil, add the onion , and stew over medium-low heat for 4-5 minutes. Add the spinach , season with salt , and cook for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally. Turn off the heat and let cool.
Meanwhile, dice the tofu . In a bowl, crack an egg, add the soy milk , and season with salt and pepper .
Mix with a whisk until you get a homogeneous mixture. Add the tofu and spinach .
Flavor with marjoram and nutmeg , then mix .
Pour everything into the pan and level. Bake in a static oven at 350°F for 60 minutes, on the lowest rack of the oven . Let it cool slightly, unmold, and serve. Your gluten-free savory pie with spinach and tofu is ready to enjoy !
The gluten-free savory pie with spinach and tofu can be stored in the refrigerator, in a sealed container or well covered with plastic wrap, for 2-3 days. If you want to keep it longer, you can freeze it whole or in portions.
You can replace the spinach with Swiss chard, kale, turnip greens, or grilled zucchini, so you have a variation for every season. If you want a more rustic base, try incorporating a small amount of buckwheat flour or fine cornmeal into the dough.
