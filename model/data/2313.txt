name:
Garganelli with cuttlefish and pea ragout
ingredients:
Egg Garganelli pasta: 8.8oz(250 g)
Peas: 1 ½cup(200 g)
Cuttlefish: 14.1oz(400 g)- (already cleaned)
Shallot: 3.5oz(100 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: 4tbsp(60 g)
Tomato purée: 1cup(250 g)
White wine: 1.8oz(50 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 40 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:

To prepare garganelli with cuttlefish and pea ragout, start by cleaning the cuttlefish by rinsing them under a stream of fresh water. Then separate the tentacles from the body and finely chop both . Also finely chop the shallot .
Pour the oil into a large non-stick pan, heat slightly and then add the shallot and the whole peeled garlic clove . Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking .
Once the shallot has softened, remove the garlic . Add the cuttlefish and mix them to blend with the other ingredients . Season with salt and pepper.
Then deglaze with white wine and add the tomato puree . Stir the ingredients and let cook for 15 minutes over moderate heat.
Finally, add the peas and let them cook for another 10-15 minutes over moderate heat. Meanwhile, boil the pasta in a pot with plenty of salted water . When it is al dente, drain it with a slotted spoon directly into the pan with the sauce .
Mix the pasta with the ragout so that it blends with the sauce . Then plate your garganelli with cuttlefish and pea ragout and enjoy !
It's recommended to consume garganelli with cuttlefish and pea ragout immediately after preparation. If you have leftovers, you can store them in an airtight container for up to 1 day.
Freezing is not recommended.
How to make these garganelli with cuttlefish and pea ragout even more flavorful? By adding a pinch of chili pepper: simply irresistible!
