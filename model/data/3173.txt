name:
Pandoro chocolate truffles
ingredients:
Pandoro pastry: 3 Â¼cups(400 g)
Orange peel: 1
Fresh liquid cream: 0.4cup(100 ml)
Unsweetened cocoa powder: 1cup(120 g)
Dark chocolate: 1.75oz(50 g)
Fresh liquid cream: 3.3tbsp(50 ml)
Chopped hazelnuts: to taste
Chopped almonds: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 20 min
Makes: 20 pieces
Cost: Very low

instructions:
To prepare the pandoro chocolate truffles, crumble the pandoro with your hands , add the grated zest of an untreated orange and the liquid cream .
Knead with your hands until you obtain a moldable mixture . Sift some unsweetened cocoa powder on a tray and start forming balls, taking about 0.7 oz of mixture for each .
Roll each ball in the unsweetened cocoa and place them in small paper cups . When you have finished the mixture, prepare the ganache for the coating: melt the dark chocolate in a bain-marie or microwave and pour in the cream that you have heated until just before boiling .
Mix the cream and chocolate well , then with a teaspoon pour a little ganache over the pandoro truffles and decorate them with coarsely chopped almonds or hazelnuts : here are your pandoro chocolate truffles!
Store the pandoro truffles in the refrigerator covered for 3-4 days. Freezing is not recommended.
For this preparation, you can also use panettone and for the ganache, you can also use white chocolate. For decoration, if you wish, you can also use pistachios or chocolate flakes.
