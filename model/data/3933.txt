name:
Stuffed Lamb Shoulder Tuscan Style
ingredients:
Lamb: 1.3lbs(600 g)- boneless shoulder
Sausage: 4.2oz(120 g)
Bread: 1.8oz(50 g)- crumb
Eggs: 1- small
Sage: 5leaves
Rosemary: 2sprigs
Garlic: 2cloves
Carrots: 2
Onions: 2
Celery: 2ribs
Extra virgin olive oil: 4 ½tbsp(60 g)
Red wine: ¼cup(50 g)
Black pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Meat broth: 0.4cup(100 g)
Lamb: to taste- the shoulder scraps
Celery: 1rib
Carrots: 1
Onions: 1
Red wine: 1.75oz(50 g)
Water: 4 ¼glasses(1 l)

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 1 h 10 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

To prepare the Tuscan-style stuffed lamb shoulder, start with the meat broth. Debone the lamb shoulder and remove all the fat. Roughly chop the celery, carrot, and onion and place them in a large pot along with the bones. Cook on high heat for 3-4 minutes, then deglaze with the red wine . Pour in the water ,
add the salt and pepper, and cook on low heat for 1 hour. Meanwhile, prepare the filling. Finely chop the carrot, celery, and onion; sauté them in a non-stick saucepan with 2 tbsps of olive oil , stirring with a kitchen spatula . Once done, turn off the heat and let them cool slightly.
Finely chop the sage, garlic, and a sprig of rosemary . Take the sausage, cut it in half with a knife, and gently remove the casing with your fingers . Place it in a bowl, mash it with a fork, and add the now-cooled vegetables .
Put the bread crumbs in a mixer with blades and blend everything , then add it to the sausage and vegetable mixture
along with an egg and part of the chopped aromatic mix . Season with salt and pepper and mix to blend all the ingredients well. Take the lamb shoulder and pound it well with a meat mallet ;
sprinkle it with the garlic and aromatic mixture you prepared , season with salt and pepper, then spread the meat and vegetable filling evenly . With your hands, roll up the meat
and How to tie a roast to prevent the filling from spilling out during cooking . In a pan, pour 2 tbsps of oil and the remaining garlic clove, place the lamb shoulder and brown it well on both sides along with a sprig of rosemary, then deglaze with the red wine .
Pour in the broth you prepared and the roughly chopped vegetables (23-24).
Cover with a lid and let cook over moderately low heat for about 1 hour, adding broth when necessary. Once cooked , place the cooking liquid and vegetables in a tall container
and blend everything to obtain a delicious accompanying sauce which you will filter and heat for a few minutes in a small saucepan (29-30).
At this point, remove the tying twine and cut the lamb shoulder into slices ; place the slices on a serving dish, drizzling them with the accompanying sauce, and serve your Tuscan-style stuffed lamb shoulder hot .
You can store the Tuscan-style stuffed lamb shoulder in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. It can also be frozen if the meat is fresh.
If you want a thicker sauce, you can add 1 tbsp of cornstarch with some cold broth; then bring to a boil to thicken everything and serve!
