name:
Monkfish Curry
ingredients:
Rice: 1 ¾cup(320 g)- for salad
Cinnamon sticks: 1
Cloves: 3
Star anise: 2
Lemons: 1
Lemon peel: 1
Cinnamon powder: ½tsp
Extra virgin olive oil: 2spoonfuls
Monkfish: 1.3lbs(600 g)- cleaned
Curry: 2spoonfuls
White onions: 1
Garlic: 2cloves
Fresh ginger: 1oz(30 g)
Fresh chili pepper: 1
Coconut milk: 1.7cups(400 g)
Parsley: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste

To prepare the monkfish curry, in a saucepan full of water add the lemon cut in half, the cinnamon stick, cloves, and star anise . Bring to a boil, heat for 15 minutes, and then keep warm. In the meantime, deal with the monkfish. If necessary, remove the central bone and skin , then cut into pieces and set aside .
Peel the onion and chop it , also chop the chili pepper after removing the seeds . Peel the ginger .
Cut it into pieces . Remove spices and aromas from the flavored water . Then in a pan pour a bit of oil, the onion, and the garlic .
Season with curry , chili pepper, and ginger , then pour a ladle of flavored water .
Add the monkfish to the pan , salt, and raise the heat trying to brown it on all sides. Pour the coconut milk and let it cook for 10 minutes over medium-low heat seasoning with chopped parsley .
Meanwhile, boil the rice for 15 minutes in the flavored boiling water .  Once cooked, drain it and season with two tablespoons of oil, lemon zest, and half a teaspoon of cinnamon. Pour the rice into bowls and accompany it with the monkfish curry .
We recommend consuming the monkfish curry immediately.
Instead of coconut milk, you can use Greek yogurt.
