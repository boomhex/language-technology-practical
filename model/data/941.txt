name:
Risotto with pumpkin, chestnuts and gorgonzola
ingredients:
Carnaroli rice: 1.67cups(320 g)
Neapolitin pumpkin: 14.1oz(400 g)
Gorgonzola cheese: 5.5oz(160 g)
Precooked chestnuts: 2.5oz(70 g)
Butter: 0.6cup(140 g)
Parmigiano Reggiano PDO cheese: 1.5oz(40 g)
White wine: 1.75oz(50 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Thyme: to taste

instructions:
Pumpkin risotto with chestnuts and gorgonzola is, you know, a perfect example of the amazing flavors Lombardy offers, especially when it’s cooler outside. This involves stirring in butter and cheese to achieve that creamy texture that just makes each bite feel extra cozy. The bold gorgonzola cheese , a local gem, adds a robust kick and totally complements the mild pumpkin and rich chestnuts beautifully.

To prepare the risotto with pumpkin, chestnuts, and gorgonzola, the first thing to do is to cut the Neapolitan pumpkin into slices about 0.4 inches thick . Remove the skin and cut into cubes about 0.4 inches large . In a pot, heat some water that will be used for cooking the risotto. Melt 5 tablespoons of butter in a pot .
Once melted, add the rice and toast it for a few minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally . Deglaze with white wine , stir, and let it evaporate completely.
Add the now hot water until it covers the rice . Add the pumpkin , a pinch of salt, and pepper .
Cook the rice by adding more hot water as needed and stirring occasionally. Meanwhile, cut the chestnuts into small pieces and add them when the rice is cooked .
Mix well and turn off the heat . Add the gorgonzola and the remaining butter .
Add the parmesan cheese and cream the risotto by stirring well . Plate the rice, add a few thyme leaves, and serve it very hot .
The risotto can be stored in the refrigerator for a couple of days, closed in an airtight container.
Flavor the risotto with some chopped rosemary.
Instead of water, you can use a delicate vegetable broth to cook the rice.
