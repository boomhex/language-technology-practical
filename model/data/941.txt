name:
Monkfish in Broth
ingredients:
Monkfish: 0.9lb(400 g)
Tomato purée: 2.1cups(500 g)
White wine: ¼cup(50 g)
Shallot: 1
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Baking soda: 1pinch
Garlic: 2cloves
Parsley: to taste- minced

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 45 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:
To make the monkfish in broth, first start by preparing the sauce. Clean and chop the shallot . In a saucepan, pour the finely chopped shallot, oil, and garlic . Stew the shallot over low heat for about 5-6 minutes. Pour in the tomato sauce
add salt, and a pinch of baking soda to remove the acidity from the tomato . Lower the heat and let it cook for 25-30 minutes with the lid on . After 20 minutes, cut the monkfish in half
and remove the central bone . Remove any skin residues and cut it into pieces of the same size, about an inch .
In another pan, pour a drizzle of oil and the garlic, heat over low heat to flavor . Remove the garlic and place the monkfish bites .
Splash with white wine and let it evaporate. Meanwhile, remove the garlic clove from the sauce and add the fish .
Adjust with salt and pepper, cover with a lid and let it cook for about 8-10 minutes. Garnish with finely chopped parsley . Serve the monkfish in broth hot with some toasted bread .
The monkfish in broth can be stored in the refrigerator for one day. You can freeze it if you used fresh fish.
If you have leftover broth, use it to dress the Pasta and fish stew : you'll get a rich and flavorful recovery dish!
. Can I use fresh tomatoes instead of the sauce?
You can try, bearing in mind that the broth will be more liquid.
. What can I use instead of shallot?
You can use white onion for flavoring.
. What is the function of baking soda?
Baking soda reduces the acidity of the tomato sauce without altering the taste.
. Can the dish be made spicy?
Sure, just add some fresh chili pepper to the shallot stir-fry.
. Is the wine essential?
The wine adds an aromatic note to the dish; if you don't like it, you can omit it.
. Why is it called monkfish?
The curious name refers to a distinctive feature of this fish, characterized by a fin above the mouth that resembles a fishing rod.
