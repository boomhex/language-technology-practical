name:
Risotto with Fennel and Primosale Cheese
ingredients:
Rice: 1 ½cup(320 g)- for risottos
Fennel: 2lbs(900 g)- cleaned 450 g
Primosale cheese: 5.5oz(160 g)
Yellow onions: 1
Almond slices: 0.44cup(40 g)
Sage: to taste
Thyme: to taste
Bay leaves: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4

instructions:
To prepare the fennel and primosale risotto, first, clean the fennel keeping aside the scraps . Then slice them thinly . Divide the onion in half .
Finely chop one of the halves of the onion . Pour the fennel cleaning scraps and the other half of the onion into a pot full of water . Bring to a boil. Meanwhile, pour some olive oil and the chopped onion into a pan .
Sauté the onion for a few minutes . Also, add the sliced fennel and sauté in the pan until golden .
Transfer one third of the cooked fennel to a bowl and set it aside for the dish decoration . Pour the rice into the pan with the cooked fennel , add salt and toast over high heat for a couple of minutes, then cover everything with the broth made from the scraps .
Also add the aromatic herbs . Cook the rice, adding broth as needed, for about 20 minutes. Slice the primosale . In a pan, toast the almond flakes .
After the cooking time, turn off the heat and cream the risotto with the crumbled primosale , stir , and finish with a drizzle of olive oil . Plate by decorating with the fennel set aside, the toasted almonds, and a few sage leaves. The fennel and primosale risotto is ready to be enjoyed.
The fennel and primosale risotto can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days in an airtight container. We do not recommend freezing it.
For a more aromatic touch, you can deglaze the risotto with white wine during the toasting.
