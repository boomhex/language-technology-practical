name:
Pasta with ricotta cream and fava beans
ingredients:
Bow tie pasta: 11.3oz(320 g)- ridged
Fava beans: 8.8oz(250 g)- Already shelled
Cow's milk ricotta cheese: 1cup(250 g)
Yellow onions: 1.4oz(40 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Marjoram: 1sprig

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 15 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:
To prepare the pasta with ricotta cream and fava beans, first, put a pot with plenty of salted water on the stove to cook the pasta. Shell the fava beans and transfer them to a saucepan with water. Bring everything to a boil and cook for another 2 minutes ; this way, the fava beans will soften, and it will be easier to peel them. At this point, drain them, transfer them to a container, and let them cool .
Using a small knife, peel the fava beans and collect them in another small bowl . At this point, add the pasta and cook it a couple of minutes less than the time indicated on the package.
Finely chop the onion , heat a bit of oil in a pan, add the onion , season with salt, add a grind of black pepper, and lightly brown. Add the peeled fava beans ,
stir and let them flavor for a minute , turn off the heat. At this point, transfer the ricotta to a bowl, add a ladle of cooking water and mix well . You should obtain a smooth and homogeneous mixture. The amount of water will be adjusted based on the type of ricotta.
As soon as the bow-tie pasta is cooked, drain it and transfer it to a bowl. Add the ricotta cream and mix. Then add the fava beans, keeping some aside for garnishing the dish, and stir again. Also add a few marjoram leaves and mix.
Transfer everything back into the pan where you cooked the fava beans , let it heat for a moment to flavor everything . Transfer to serving plates, decorating with the reserved fava beans and a few marjoram leaves . The ricotta cream and fava bean pasta is ready, serve it immediately .
We recommend consuming it immediately. Alternatively, it can be stored in the refrigerator for up to one day.
