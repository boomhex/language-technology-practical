name:
Savory cream with mushrooms and bacon
ingredients:
Smoked pancetta: 3.5oz(100 g)- (1 thick slice)
Asiago cheese: 5.3oz(150 g)
Champignon mushrooms: 3 ½cups(250 g)
Egg yolks: 5
Fresh liquid cream: ¾cup(200 g)
Whole milk: 1.7cups(400 g)
Saffron: 1bag- (0.1 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Eggs: 1- (medium)

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 1 h 10 min
Makes: 10 pieces
Cost: Average

To make the savory cream with mushrooms and bacon, first cut the Asiago into cubes and then chop it with a knife . Transfer the cheese into a small pot, pour in the milk and cream
Add a pinch of salt , the saffron powder , and stir with a spoon .
Transfer the pot to the heat and cook the mixture on low flame for about 15-20 minutes, stirring often. You should obtain a smooth cream not very thick, set it aside to cool. Now clean the mushrooms: remove the earthy part of the stem and gently wipe them with a cloth .
Thinly slice the mushrooms and then cut the bacon into cubes . Pour a dash of oil into a pan
Add the bacon cubes here and transfer everything to the heat, warm over low heat and then add the mushrooms . Continue cooking for 10 minutes. Season with pepper and set aside.
In a bowl, beat the yolks of 5 eggs with the whole egg , add the mixture to the now cold cream , and mix well to combine .
Now distribute the mixture into oven-proof cocottes , place them on a baking sheet and bake in a preheated ventilated oven at 300°F for 20 minutes on the middle shelf. After this time, remove the cocottes
Fill them with mushrooms and bacon (22-23) and put back in the oven for another 20 minutes. After this time, the savory cream with mushrooms and bacon will be ready to be enjoyed .
The savory cream with mushrooms and bacon can be stored in the fridge for about 2 days. It can be frozen either before or after cooking once cooled. Just bake it directly in a hot oven.
Let the cream, milk, and cheese mixture cool very well before adding it to the eggs to avoid unpleasant lumps. Asiago can be replaced with fontina or provolone.
