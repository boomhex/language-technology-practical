name:
Puff pastries with olives, Jerusalem artichoke, and raspadura
ingredients:
Crispy baked puff pastry: 12- with olives
Jerusalem artichoke: 5oz(140 g)
Raspadura cheese: - 12 flakes
Vegetable broth: 0.4cup(100 g)
Parsley: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 20 min
Makes: 12 pieces
Cost: Low

instructions:
To prepare the puff pastries with olives, Jerusalem artichoke, and raspadura, start by cleaning the Jerusalem artichoke, removing the outer skin with a small knife , then cut it into very small cubes . Next, peel the garlic and chop it , you can also sauté it whole in the pan if you prefer to remove it later.
Also finely chop a bunch of parsley then in a large pan, pour a drizzle of oil and add the Jerusalem artichoke and the garlic whole or chopped.
Salt and pepper to taste and mix. Add the parsley then pour in the vegetable broth or hot water to aid cooking. Let it cook for about 20 minutes over moderate heat, until the Jerusalem artichoke cubes are softened.
When the Jerusalem artichoke is cooked, turn off the heat and on a cutting board arrange the olive puff pastries. Add a slice of raspadura (you can also shave thin slices directly from the Grana cheese). On each pastry also add a teaspoon of sautéed Jerusalem artichoke and if you wish, you can garnish with a pinch of chopped parsley before serving the puff pastries with olives, Jerusalem artichoke, and raspadura!
The puff pastries with olives, Jerusalem artichoke, and raspadura are excellent if consumed immediately. The sautéed Jerusalem artichokes can be stored in the refrigerator covered with plastic wrap for 1-2 days.
