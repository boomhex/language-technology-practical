name:
Pasta with Monkfish, Zucchini, and Sun-Dried Tomatoes
ingredients:
Fusilli bucati corti pasta: 11.3oz(320 g)
Monkfish: 1.1lbs(500 g)
Zucchini: 3 ½cups(380 g)
Dried tomatoes: 2.6oz(75 g)
Shallot: 1.25oz(35 g)
White wine: 1.75oz(50 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: 2tbsp(30 g)
Parsley: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Water: ½cup(100 g)

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:

To prepare the pasta with monkfish, zucchini, and sun-dried tomatoes, start by cutting the monkfish into cubes about 3/4 inch . Trim the zucchini and slice them into thin rounds , then cut the sun-dried tomatoes into small pieces .
Finely chop the shallot and the parsley . Proceed with the preparation of the sauce: take a non-stick pan with a wide base and drizzle it with a little oil. Then, let the shallot sauté over low heat for about 4-5 minutes .
Add the tomatoes and 2 oz of water and let cook over low heat for another 5 minutes .
When they have softened, add the zucchini , adjust with salt and pepper, and mix them with the other ingredients. Pour the remaining 2 oz of water and let cook for 10 minutes over low heat.
Now add the monkfish and mix it with the other ingredients . Deglaze with white wine , letting it cook for 3-4 minutes.
Pour in the parsley , taking care to set aside a little for the final decoration of the dish, and mix the ingredients . Now cook the pasta in plenty of salted water and drain it al dente.
Transfer it to the pan with the sauce and sauté for 2-3 minutes. Sprinkle with the remaining parsley and serve your pasta with monkfish, zucchini, and sun-dried tomatoes !
It is recommended to consume the pasta with monkfish, zucchini, and sun-dried tomatoes immediately after preparation. However, you can store it once cooked in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
You can also freeze the fish sauce if you have used only fresh ingredients and not defrosted ones.
To flavor this delicately tasting sauce, you can opt for thyme and marjoram as a substitute for parsley. For a bold touch, try adding a spicy chili pepper!
The anglerfish is also called monkfish. It is often found at the fishmonger already headless (since the head contains many bones), especially in winter months.
