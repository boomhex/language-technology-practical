name:
Melon Cube
ingredients:
Cantaloupe: 1- small
Melon: 1- small winter
Watermelon: 1- small baby
Mint: to taste
Water: 1cup(250 g)
Fresh ginger: 1.5oz(40 g)
Sugar: 1 ⅛cup(225 g)

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 10 min
Makes: 2 pieces
Cost: Average
Note + the cooling time of the syrup

instructions:

To prepare the melon cube, start with the ginger syrup. Peel the ginger and cut it into pieces in a saucepan with water , also add the sugar
and turn on the stove. Let the mixture come to a simmer, then let it gently simmer until it reaches a viscous consistency . At the end of cooking, add mint leaves


and let it cool completely . Once it reaches room temperature, strain and collect the syrup in a serving bowl .
In the meantime, you can move on to the melons and watermelon. Remove the rind from all three and cut into equal cubes of about 3/4 inch . It's important that they are as similar as possible in shape to respect the geometry .
Now move on to forming the two cubes. On the serving plate, arrange the cubes next to each other , forming a square base of 9 cubes , and continue in this manner for 2 more layers, trying to alternate the fruits as much as possible ,
in this way you will get a replica of the Rubik's Cube . Accompany the fruit cube with the sauce and a small fork to dip the fruit tips into it . Your melon cube is ready, garnish with some mint leaves and enjoy .
Once the melon cube is ready, it is recommended to consume it immediately and not store it.
However, you can prepare the accompanying sauce a day in advance and store it in a cool place, covered with plastic wrap.
Along with the melon cube, you can create other flavors, especially with exotic fruits: this way, you can take a trip around the world! You will surely have some leftover fruit, and since you have already cut it, you can make quick fruit juices or a fresh and inviting Watermelon Fruit Bowl !
