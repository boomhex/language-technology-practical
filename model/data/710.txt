name:
Risotto with bell peppers, peanuts, and cheese
ingredients:
Vialone nano rice: 1.67cups(320 g)
Red peppers: 5 ⅓cups(800 g)
Garlic: 1clove
Roasted salted peanuts: ½cup(70 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Sun-dried tomatoes in oil: 1.5oz(40 g)
Parmigiano Reggiano PDO cheese: 0.4cup(40 g)
Mint: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Fresh chili pepper: to taste
Tomini cheese: 4

instructions:
In Piedmont, Northern Italy, risotto with peppers gets a surprising twist with peanuts and a layer of creamy grilled tomino cheese. And not your typical bell pepper risotto. No, it's not. Instead, you get tender rice that soaks up the flavors from sweet red peppers, then—bam!—a crunchy twist from the peanuts. And the cheese? Local tomino melts smoothly over everything, adding that moist richness everyone loves around here.

The mix of melted cheese, nutty peanuts, and roasted peppers might seem bold, but honestly, it works beautifully. In Piedmont, some folks mix it up with burrata instead of tomino or toss in some arugula for a tangy bite—plenty of room for creativity.
Here's the thing: the risotto with peanuts is what makes it special, adding a crunch you never knew you needed in a creamy risotto . And look, it's the kind of dish you'd find at a family table in Piedmont, where people love playing with new flavors while still nailing the traditional risotto technique. That makes the rice soft and satisfying, for sure.
Compared to regular vegetarian risotto, this version feels adventurous—like a dish full of surprises in every bite. It’s really really exciting to try it out. You get the blend of golden peppers, peanuts, and melted cheese that turns a classic Italian favorite into something totally fresh and super tasty.

To prepare the risotto with bell peppers, peanuts, and cheese, wash the bell peppers, remove the cap , and then cut them in half lengthwise. Open them up and remove the seeds and filaments . Salt a grill pan, and when it's hot, roast the bell peppers and garlic , turning them occasionally, without being afraid to char the skin.
When they are soft and smoky , remove them from the heat, place them in a container , and cover with plastic wrap. Let them cool down .
Grind the peanuts to obtain a coarse powder to set aside. Peel the bell peppers and remove the garlic's skin .
In the same mixer, add the bell peppers, garlic , some mint leaves, salt, chili pepper , and oil .
Add the drained sun-dried tomatoes and blend the mixture . Toast the rice dry , then add salted hot water and continue cooking, adding a little at a time.
When the rice is almost ready, add the bell pepper cream , finish cooking, and off the heat, stir in the Parmigiano Reggiano and add the chopped peanuts .
Grill the tominos on a hot grill for about 2 minutes, then plate the risotto adding a tomino to each portion , some chopped peanuts , chili pepper to taste, and a few mint leaves . The risotto with bell peppers, peanuts, and cheese is ready to be served.
The risotto with bell peppers, peanuts, and cheese can be stored for one day in the refrigerator without the tomino. You can prepare the bell pepper cream in advance and store it in the fridge.
You can also use this bell pepper cream to season pasta or spread it on toasted bread crostini.
