name:
Stewed Cardoons
ingredients:
Thistles: 2.2lbs(1 kg)
Fresh scallion: 3
Thyme: 4sprigs-
Extra virgin olive oil: 3spoonfuls-
Smoked pancetta: 5oz(140 g)
Dry white wine: 1glass-
Cinnamon powder: 1pinch-
Parmigiano Reggiano PDO cheese: 1.4oz(40 g)
Lemons: 1-
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Tomato purée: 4spoonfuls

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 50 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:

Start preparing the stewed cardoons by cleaning the vegetable in this way: cut off the end of the cardoon bunch and detach each stalk, removing the leaves. If the cardoon stalks are too wide, cut them in half lengthwise and then use a knife to score one end and pull from the opposite side to remove the strings. Repeat the operation starting from the opposite end. Once all the strings are removed, cut the cardoon into pieces about 6 inches long and place them in a bowl of acidulated water (i.e., with the addition of the juice of 1 lemon) to prevent them from darkening. When you have cleaned and cut all the cardoons, rinse them under cold running water and boil them in a pot full of water with a tablespoon of coarse salt for about 15 minutes if using a pressure cooker, or for about 25-30 minutes if boiling traditionally.
Once cooked, drain the cardoons. In a separate pan, place the oil and scallions and let them soften over low heat for about 15 minutes; add chopped bacon , thyme, and cinnamon and let them sauté for a few minutes, then add the cardoons , stir gently, and add the white wine, allowing it to evaporate.
Adjust with salt and pepper , add the tomato puree , and possibly a ladle of hot water, then let cook until all the ingredients are flavored and the cooking base is reduced. Add the grated Parmesan cheese and serve the stewed cardoons while still hot.
