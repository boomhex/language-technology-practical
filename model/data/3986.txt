name:
Mustard (Dijon Mustard)
ingredients:
Black grapes: 2.5lbs(500 g)
Apples: 2
Lemon peel: 1
Red wine: 1glass
White mustard powder: 3tbsp(40 g)

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 2 h 10 min
Serving: 4

instructions:

First, wash and dry the grapes, then process them through a food mill to obtain the juice, which you will filter through a sieve. Then cut the two apples into slices and cook them with the wine until it is completely absorbed. At this point, add the grape juice and cook it until it becomes thick, stirring the mixture often to prevent it from sticking. At the end of cooking, remove the pot from the heat and add the mustard, which you have previously dissolved in a bit of warm red wine, and the lemon zest. Let it cool for a few minutes and jar your mustard while it's still warm in airtight jars if you wish to preserve it, or serve it as a condiment for your dishes.
Although this is the original mustard recipe, it is not widely followed and used in Italy because the Mantuan or Cremonese mustard, which are variants of the original, is preferred. In Sicily, however, this mustard is widely used, and in ancient times, it was distributed to neighbors during the grape harvest or served as a dessert during baptisms, confirmations, and engagements. In France, there are different ways to prepare mustard depending on the city: the most famous, Dijon mustard, is the spiciest of all, then there is Bordeaux mustard, relatively spicy but tends to be sweet, and finally, there is Mieux mustard, which is the sweetest.
