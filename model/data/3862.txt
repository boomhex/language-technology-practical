name:
Chanterelle and Gruyere Crostini
ingredients:
Bread: 8slices
Parsley: 1tuft
Garlic: 1clove
Extra virgin olive oil: 3spoonfuls
Fine salt: to taste
Chanterelle mushrooms (chanterelle): 5.3oz(150 g)
Gruyere cheese: 8spoonfuls- to grate
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 10 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average
Note Plus 5 minutes of grilling

instructions:

To prepare the chanterelle and Gruyere crostini, wash the chanterelle mushrooms very quickly under cold running water and dry them with a clean cloth (1-2). Alternatively, if they are not very dirty, wipe them with a damp and soft cloth. Cut the larger chanterelles into smaller pieces .
In a pan, place the extra virgin olive oil and the well-crushed garlic , sauté for a minute, and add the chanterelles , which you will cook over low heat for 5 minutes. Add the finely chopped parsley ,
cook for another minute, finish cooking, and season with salt and pepper . Meanwhile, grate the Gruyere and cut 8 slices about 1-1.5 inches thick from a baguette .
Place the bread slices on a baking sheet , turn on the grill, and bake them for a minute until they are toasted. At this point, place some Gruyere cheese on each crostini and gratinate for another minute, then place a spoonful of chanterelles on each bread slice, and you will be ready to serve your chanterelle and Gruyere crostini.
Chanterelle and Gruyere crostini should be consumed immediately while still hot. You can prepare in advance by cooking the chanterelles first and storing them in a covered container in the refrigerator for 2 days. When ready to assemble the crostini, simply warm them up. Freezing is not recommended.
You can also prepare these crostini with different mushrooms, such as porcini, honey mushrooms, or button mushrooms.
For the correct selection, treatment, and storage of mushrooms, refer to the Ministry of Health's guidelines .
