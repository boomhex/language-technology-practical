name:
Danish meatballs (Frikadeller) with parsley potatoes
ingredients:
Pork: 1.1lbs(500 g)- ground
Breadcrumbs: 0.67cup(80 g)
Yellow onions: 7oz(200 g)
Eggs: 2
Blond beer: 7oz(200 g)- Danish at room temperature
Extra virgin olive oil: 2.4tbsp(35 g)
Lemons: 2.1oz(60 g)
Nutmeg: ½tsp- grated
Cinnamon powder: ½tsp
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Potatoes: 0.9lb(400 g)
Parsley: 2tufts
Extra virgin olive oil: 2tbsp(30 g)
Butter: 3 ½tbsp(50 g)
Type 00 flour: 10spoonfuls

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 35 min
Cook time: 10 min
Makes: 38 pieces
Cost: Low

instructions:

To prepare Danish meatballs (Frikadeller) with parsley potatoes, put a pot of salted water on the stove which you will use for the Vegetable broth . Finely chop the onion and let it soften in a non-stick pan together with olive oil for 15 minutes over very low heat , occasionally adding vegetable broth .
Turn off the heat and set aside in a bowl to cool. Place the minced meat in a bowl, add the eggs , the cinnamon ,
the grated nutmeg and adjust the salt and pepper .
Mix all the ingredients well by working the mixture with your hands , then add the breadcrumbs and continue kneading. Also add the softened, now cool onion ,
mix everything with the help of a spatula until you obtain a homogeneous mixture that you will let rest for 10 minutes . After the necessary time, form balls weighing 0.7-0.9 oz each with the mixture you have prepared, rolling them between the palms of your hands to give them a round shape and proceed this way until you finish the mixture: with our doses, you should obtain 38 meatballs.
Roll them in flour , turning them so that it adheres evenly. Once finished, melt the butter in a large pan and cook the meatballs for 2 minutes over high heat ,
turning them for even cooking . Pour the room temperature beer and lemon juice
and cook over low heat for another 3-4 minutes until the sauce thickens . Turn off, keep the meatballs warm and in the meantime prepare the potato side dish. Wash them under plenty of fresh running water, then boil them with the skin on for 30 minutes . Afterwards, peel them
and slice them ; serve your Danish meatballs (Frikadeller) with parsley potatoes finishing with a pinch of salt and a sprinkle of parsley and a drizzle of oil .
You can store Danish meatballs (Frikadeller) with parsley potatoes for a maximum of 2-3 days in the refrigerator in an airtight container. You can freeze the raw meatballs if you have used fresh products.
If you prefer, instead of dark beer, you can use a light one.
