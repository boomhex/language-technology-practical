name:
Rose Petal Jelly
ingredients:
Rose wine: 1.7cups(400 g)- petals
Lemon juice: 1
Water: 2.1cups(500 ml)
Sugar: 3cups(600 g)
Anise seeds: 1tsp(5 g)
Rose water: 4spoonfuls

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4

instructions:

First, gather roses that are fully bloomed but not wilted to ensure the flowers have not already lost their fragrance, which is the main feature of this jelly. Choose roses that are, if possible, red or pink, fragrant, and fleshy. Remove the petals by detaching the base and crush them in a mortar along with anise seeds. Then put the resulting mixture in a blender, add half of the sugar, and blend until you get a pulverized mixture of sugar and petals. Pour everything into a stainless steel pot, add the remaining sugar, lemon juice, rose water, water, and bring to a boil. Cook over moderate heat, stirring occasionally and skimming if necessary, for 25-30 minutes or until a drop of the mixture, dropped on a plate, remains firm. Finally, pour the jelly into jars while still hot, seal tightly, and store in a cool place until use. Consume after at least a month.
Rose petal jelly is, as we have already mentioned, a preserve with a very particular flavor that can be successfully enjoyed in combination with any food. In any case, the perfect pairing seems to be with cheeses, and in particular with fresh robiola cheese, so that the sweet flavor of the jelly and the creamy and slightly tangy flavor of the cheese meet.
The color of rose petal jelly can vary greatly depending on the variety and color of the roses chosen: it will have a more amber hue if red roses are chosen, less amber if pink roses are chosen.
