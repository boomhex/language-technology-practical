name:
Bavette with citrus pesto
ingredients:
Linguine pasta: 11.25oz(320 g)
Basil: 3cups(60 g)
Lemons: 4oz(110 g)- (about 1)
Oranges: 1.04lbs(470 g)- (about 2)
Extra virgin olive oil: 8tbsp(120 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Pine nuts: ¼cup(30 g)
Grana Padano PDO cheese: 2.1oz(60 g)- grated

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 10 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:
To prepare the bavette with citrus pesto, start by peeling the citrus fruits: with a paring knife, remove the ends of the oranges, down to the flesh; then remove the peel including the white part, following the curve of the segment ; extract the segments one by one to free them from the skin that covers them , proceed the same way with the lemons: remove the ends, then the peel and the white part
then extract the segments as you did with the oranges. Now take the basil leaves (you can clean them with a dry cloth or wash them in a bowl with cold water, very gently; then dry them always gently with kitchen paper) and place them in a mixer with blades , add the pine nuts ,
the grated cheese , the orange segments , and the lemon segments .
Finally, drizzle in the olive oil and operate the processor to blend the mixture until smooth . Meanwhile, in a large pot, bring water to a boil, salt it, and cook the bavette ; cook them al dente.
Meanwhile, pour the pesto into a large pan or a sauté pan, salt and pepper to taste.

When the bavette are cooked, drain them directly into the pan with the pesto . Sauté the bavette with citrus pesto for a few moments and then serve them hot .
The bavette with citrus pesto can be stored in the refrigerator in an airtight container for up to one day. Freezing is not recommended.
You can store the citrus pesto in the refrigerator in a jar, covered with olive oil, for up to 3-4 days.
Want to try a more intense flavor variant? Replace the basil with arugula and the pine nuts with almonds, success is guaranteed!
