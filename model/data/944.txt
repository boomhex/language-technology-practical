name:
Fried Cod Roman Style (in batter)
ingredients:
Desalted cod: 1lb(465 g)
Type 00 flour: 2.1cups(300 g)
Sparkling water: 1.6cups(370 g)- (cold)
Fine salt: to taste
Sunflower seed oil: 4 ¼cups(1 l)

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 10 min
Makes: 6 pieces
Cost: Low
Note + the resting time of the batter in the fridge

instructions:
To prepare the fried cod Roman style (in batter), make sure the cod is well desalted, otherwise follow our " How to Desalt Cod " guide. Pat the desalted cod dry with paper towels , then remove the skin using a knife .
Also remove the bones using tweezers . Then cut the cod into 8 pieces of approximately the same size and set them aside for a moment. In a bowl, pour in the flour and sparkling water gradually .
Mix with a whisk until you obtain a dense and covering batter. Heat the vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot; when it reaches 375°F, it's time to fry. Dip 2-3 pieces of cod in the batter and immediately after, plunge them into the oil .
Fry for about 8 minutes, turning the pieces occasionally and frequently checking the oil temperature. Once cooked, drain on paper towels and season with a pinch of salt. Your fried cod Roman style (in batter) is ready to be enjoyed !
It is recommended to consume the fried cod Roman style (in batter) immediately; if there are leftovers, you can store them in the fridge for 1-2 days at most, making sure to reheat them in the oven before enjoying.
For a perfect frying of fried cod Roman style (in batter): the cod must be well desalted and dried before use, only in this way will the batter adhere perfectly. You can flavor the batter with grated lemon zest. The oil temperature is the secret to excellent frying: measure it with a thermometer and do not worry if it seems high: the cold batter will lower the temperature. For this reason, fry few pieces at a time.
