name:
Potato loaf
ingredients:
Yellow potatoes: 1.75lbs(800 g)
Eggs: 2
Fresh scallion: 2.1oz(60 g)- to clean
Red peppers: 3.5oz(100 g)- to clean
Carrots: 3.5oz(100 g)- to be cleaned
Zucchini: ¾cup(100 g)- to clean
Extra virgin olive oil: 1spoonful
Grated cheese: 2oz(60 g)
Breadcrumbs: 0.3cup(40 g)
Salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Breadcrumbs: ¼cup(30 g)- coarse
Almond slices: ¼cup(20 g)

instructions:

To prepare the potato loaf, first cook the potatoes with the skin on . Start with cold water and once it reaches a boil, cook for 25-30 minutes. Meanwhile, dice the spring onions into 1/4 inch cubes . Cut the peppers and remove the seeds and internal filaments .
Cut them into 1/4 inch cubes , do the same for the carrots and zucchinis .
In a pan, pour a drizzle of oil, add the spring onions , and season with salt . Sauté over medium-low heat for about 6 minutes. Add the pepper , increase the heat and cook for 5 minutes.
Add the carrot and cook for 2 minutes. Add the zucchini and season with salt and pepper . Continue cooking the vegetables for another 5-6 minutes.
Once the potatoes are cooked, mash them in a bowl and let them cool slightly. Then add the eggs and grated cheese .
Adjust with salt and pepper and mix briefly. Add the breadcrumbs and vegetables .
Mix to combine the ingredients . Place the mixture on parchment paper and shape it into a rectangular loaf . Sprinkle the top and sides with coarse breadcrumbs and almond flakes .
Transfer everything onto a baking tray and bake in a static oven at 350°F for 35 minutes . Your potato loaf is ready to be served .
The potato loaf can be stored in the refrigerator, in a sealed container or wrapped in plastic wrap, for 2 days. It can be frozen after cooking.
You can vary the vegetables according to your taste or seasonality. For a more indulgent touch, you can enrich it with a cheesy heart or a mozzarella filling. Besides the classic zucchinis and carrots, you can add sautéed mushrooms or spinach for a more decisive flavor. You can season with aromatic herbs like rosemary, thyme, marjoram, and sage.
