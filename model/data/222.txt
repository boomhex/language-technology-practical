name:
Blackberry and ricotta cake
ingredients:
Type 0 flour: 1.6cups(200 g)
Almond flour: 1cup(100 g)
Eggs: 3.9oz(110 g)- (about 2 medium)
Cow's milk ricotta cheese: 1cup(250 g)
Brown sugar: 0.67cup(150 g)
Dark chocolate 70%: 1.8oz(50 g)- chips
Whole milk: ¼cup(50 g)
Wildflower honey: 1tbsp(20 g)
Sunflower seed oil: ½cup(100 g)
Blackberries: 3 ¼cups(450 g)
Baking powder: 2.67tsp(12 g)
Fine salt: 1pinch
Almond slices: to taste
Powdered sugar: to taste

instructions:

Italians might enjoy it with afternoon coffee or breakfast, lovingly calling it ‘torta alla ricotta e cioccolato’—a nod to their love of dairy and chocolate in sweets.
In Southern Italy, it’s all about simplicity and flavor. And look, this ricotta cake recipe fits that perfectly. For sure. You get a cake that's tender and perfectly sweet, so it's hard to stop at just one slice. Really, really hard. Even without butter, it keeps that rich, moist ricotta cake vibe, thanks to fresh ricotta and electric whisks—no fancy tricks, just genuine Italian baking. Pretty much. Southern Italians might swap out the fruit based on what's in season, but blackberries add that tangy pop, complementing the chocolate. Thing is, if you’re craving a traditional ricotta cake , this is a fantastic choice—easy, full of flavor, and perfect for when you want a bit of Italian sweetness in your day. Cannot go wrong.

To prepare the blackberry and ricotta cake , drain the ricotta from the whey. Pour the brown sugar and whole eggs into a bowl , and whisk with electric whisks until you get a light and frothy mixture . Add the honey .
Add a pinch of salt , the drained cow's milk ricotta , and continue working with the whisks. Then, pour the vegetable oil in a thin stream while mixing .
Once the oil is absorbed, incorporate the all-purpose flour , almond flour , and sifted baking powder .
Work everything, then add the milk while continuing to mix with the whisks . Once ready , add 400 g of washed and gently patted dry blackberries .
Add the chocolate chips and mix . Line a 20 cm (8-inch) diameter cake pan with parchment paper, then pour the batter, leveling the surface with the back of a spoon . Cover with the remaining blackberries .
Sprinkle the sliced almonds and bake in a preheated static oven at 356°F (180°C) for about 45 minutes (check the baking and do the toothpick test before removing from the oven). Once baked, take the super soft ricotta cake out of the oven , let it cool, then remove it from its mold to cool completely. Serve with powdered sugar if desired .
The blackberry and ricotta cake keeps at room temperature for 2-3 days. It can be frozen after baking.
The presence of fruit might moisten the batter considerably, requiring an extra 5 minutes of baking time, always do the toothpick test to adjust accordingly. You can substitute blackberries with raspberries or blueberries.
