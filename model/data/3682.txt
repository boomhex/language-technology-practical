name:
Moroccan Filo Pastry Cigars
ingredients:
Phyllo dough: - 5 sheets
Onions: - 1/2
Pork: 1.1lbs(500 g)- minced
Cinnamon powder: 1tsp
Fresh ginger: 1- small piece
Eggs: 2
Parsley: 2spoonfuls- chopped
Black pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Butter: to taste- melted
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 50 min
Cook time: 60 min
Makes: 15 pieces
Cost: Low

instructions:

Finely chop the onion . In a large pan, sauté the onion until it becomes translucent . Add the meat and sauté until it turns golden brown .
Incorporate the cinnamon , a bit of freshly ground pepper, a pinch of salt, and the grated ginger . Beat the eggs in a separate bowl. Incorporate them into the meat and mix well until they are well combined and the mixture is creamy.
Put the meat aside in a bowl, add the finely chopped parsley, mix and let cool. Preheat the oven to 300°F. Prepare the cigars: cut the filo pastry sheets into three to obtain three rectangles of similar size. Brush one of them with melted butter , place a tablespoon of filling at one end of the filo pastry rectangle,
fold the edges lengthwise and roll the cigar onto itself, brushing the outside with melted butter . Form all the cigars and place them on a tray lined with parchment paper . Bake the cigars in a preheated oven for 45 minutes or until golden brown on both sides.
These warm Moroccan cigars are delicious served with the typical spicy Harissa sauce and a salad of carrots, raisins, and slivered almonds dressed with honey, lemon, oil, cinnamon, powdered ginger, salt, and pepper. Instead of ground beef, try using lamb!
