name:
Monkfish with olives
ingredients:
Monkfish: 1.9lbs(850 g)
Piccadilly tomatoes: 1.1lbs(500 g)
Garlic: 1clove
Extra virgin olive oil: 3 Â½tbsp(50 g)
Taggiasca olives: 6.4oz(180 g)
Basil: 4leaves
Black pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 12 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:
To prepare the monkfish with olives, start by washing and cutting the piccadilly tomatoes into wedges . Take the monkfish steak and wash it under running water, cut it into slices about 1.5 inches thick, keeping the central spine . Alternatively, you can remove it with a knife and cut the flesh into smaller pieces. In a non-stick pan, heat the extra virgin olive oil with a peeled garlic clove to flavor it , taking care to remove it afterward.
Add the piccadilly tomato wedges , salt, and pepper , and after about 5 minutes of cooking, add the Taggiasca olives .
Lastly, add the monkfish steaks to the fresh tomato sauce , adjust salt and pepper, cover with the lid , and cook for 2-3 minutes over medium heat. Turn the steaks to ensure even cooking and continue for another 2-3 minutes.
Fragrance the fish with fresh basil leaves (you can also use dried basil). The monkfish with olives is ready , serve it very hot !
It is preferable to consume the monkfish with tomato sauce freshly made. However, it can also be stored in the refrigerator for up to 1 day if placed in an airtight container. It is not recommended to freeze it.
Can't do without a bit of spice? Add some fresh or powdered chili pepper to give even more character to this dish!
