name:
Pork tenderloin with pumpkin and figs
ingredients:
Pork filet: 1.8lbs(800 g)
Rosemary: 4sprigs
Garlic: 2cloves
Sage: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Water: to taste
Pumpkin: 2cups(500 g)
Rosemary: 1sprig
Figs: 8
Honey: 1spoonful
Port wine: 0.833cup(200 ml)
Meat broth: 6 ¾tbsp(100 ml)
Shallot: 1
Butter: 1 ½tbsp(20 g)

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 50 min
Cook time: 60 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:
Pork tenderloin with pumpkin and figs is an autumnal main course perfect for those seeking an original and surprising recipe. It’s a dish full of contrasts, yet well balanced in flavors: the sweetness of the pumpkin and figs pairs perfectly with the pork. To complete the dish, a delicate Port wine sauce adds a touch of elegance and an aromatic note. It’s a recipe suitable for many occasions: from a family meal to a special dinner where you wish to serve something original and sophisticated. Pork tenderloin with pumpkin and figs will become one of your go-to dishes when you want to impress even the most discerning palates.

To prepare the pork tenderloin with pumpkin and figs, first take the pumpkin: remove the seeds, the internal fibers , and the peel . Then cut it into cubes .
In a pot, pour a drizzle of oil and add the pumpkin. Add the rosemary and sage , season with salt . Cover with water and cook for 15-20 minutes, until the pumpkin is soft.
In the meantime, trim the excess fat from the tenderloin , cut it in half, and season with salt and pepper .
In a pan, pour a drizzle of oil, add the rosemary, sage, and unpeeled garlic . Add the tenderloin and brown it for 3-4 minutes on each side .
Place the tenderloins on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper , add the rosemary , and drizzle them with the cooking juices . Bake in a static oven at 350°F for 15-20 minutes until the internal temperature of the meat reaches 150°F; you can use a probe to measure the core temperature of the meat. Once cooked, let it rest for at least 5 minutes.
Meanwhile, in a pan, pour the Port wine , add the shallot and the vegetable broth .
Cook until the liquid is reduced by half, then strain into a small pot. Add the butter and let it melt completely . Set aside.
Cut the figs in half . In a pan, pour a drizzle of oil, add the honey and place the figs cut side down . Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side. Using an immersion blender, blend the pumpkin until you get a smooth puree.
Slice the tenderloin . On the plate, spread the pumpkin puree , and place the tenderloin slices on top .
Add the figs and the Port wine sauce , finish with some rosemary . Your pork tenderloin with pumpkin and figs is ready to be served .
The pork tenderloin with pumpkin and figs can be stored in the refrigerator, in a closed container, for up to 2 days. Before consuming, you can reheat it in the oven at low temperature or in a pan with a drizzle of oil to restore crispiness to the surface.
To enrich the dish, you can add other spices such as thyme, bay leaf, pink pepper, or cumin for a more exotic touch. You can replace the Port wine sauce with a balsamic vinegar reduction or a red wine sauce.
