name:
Salmon fillets on julienne of Brussels sprouts, leeks, and carrots
ingredients:
Salmon fillets: 1.8lbs(800 g)
Brussels sprouts: 7oz(200 g)
Leeks: 5.5oz(160 g)
Carrots: 1 ½cup(140 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Thyme: to taste
Mustard: 2spoonfuls- ancient
Lemon juice: ½
Extra virgin olive oil: 3spoonfuls
Fine salt: 1pinch

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 15 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Very low

instructions:

To prepare the salmon fillets with julienne of Brussels sprouts, leeks, and carrots, start with the vegetables. Rinse the Brussels sprouts under running water , along with the carrots and leeks. Then place them on a cutting board, peel away the outer leaves of the Brussels sprouts , remove the stem with a small knife, and cut them in half. Then cut them into julienne strips .
Finely julienne the carrots and the leeks . In a large pan, pour a drizzle of oil
and add the vegetables: leeks , carrots , and finally the Brussels sprouts . Sauté the vegetables over high heat, adding a bit of water if necessary.
Salt and pepper to taste, add a bit more water if necessary to wilt the vegetables and after a few minutes, turn off the heat. Then prepare the mustard sauce to drizzle over the salmon fillets: in a small bowl, pour the whole grain mustard ,
add the oil and the juice of half a lemon . Whisk the sauce to blend the ingredients.
Salt to taste and also add a few thyme leaves . Now that the sauce is ready, prepare the salmon: place the salmon side on a cutting board and cut out 4 fillets (17-18). Remove any bones with tweezers.
Salt the fillets and place them in a large, previously oiled baking dish . Then cover them with the mustard sauce and sprinkle with a few thyme leaves . Bake the salmon fillets in a preheated static oven at 365°F for about 15 minutes (330°F for about 10 minutes if using a convection oven).
After the necessary time, remove the fillets from the oven . Take a serving plate, lay the sautéed vegetables and the salmon fillets . Serve your salmon fillets with julienne of Brussels sprouts, leeks, and carrots hot!
Store the salmon fillets with julienne of Brussels sprouts, leeks, and carrots in the refrigerator for 1-2 days, covered with plastic wrap. Freezing is not recommended
You can create the julienne vegetable mix as you prefer, also with artichokes, or zucchini to add or replace the carrots, leeks, and Brussels sprouts.
