name:
Noodle Soup
ingredients:
Beef: 8.8oz(250 g)
Noodles: 6.4oz(180 g)
Water: 3.8cups(900 g)- (1.7 cups for the meat + 2.1 cups for the broth)
Bak choi: 8leaves
Carrots: 3.5oz(100 g)
Fresh ginger: 2slices
Bay leaves: 1leaf
Cinnamon sticks: ½
Star anise: 1
Fresh chili pepper: to taste
Soy sauce: 2spoonfuls- light
Soy sauce: 1spoonful- dark
Rice wine: 2spoonfuls
Sunflower seed oil: to taste- about
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 1 h 20 min
Serving: 2
Cost: Average

To make the noodle soup, first cut the beef into slices about 1 inch thick , then reduce it to cubes . Rinse under running water .
Place the meat in a pot with 14 oz of cold water , then put it on the stove. As soon as the water comes to a boil , turn off the heat and drain . Set the meat aside.
Heat a wok over high heat, then add the seed oil. Add the ginger slices , the meat , and the fresh chili rings .
Season with light soy sauce and dark soy sauce , mix and add the bay leaf .
Add the cinnamon sticks and star anise , and season with salt and pepper.
Pour in the rice wine and stir-fry everything for 5 minutes. Meanwhile, heat 17.6 oz of water in a separate saucepan. Pour the hot water into the wok and bring to a boil; then, cover with a lid and cook on low heat for 50-60 minutes.
In the meantime, prepare the vegetables: remove the base of the pak choi and separate the leaves , then peel the carrots and cut them into oblique slices .
Bring a pot of water to a boil, add the vegetables and blanch them for one minute, then drain and set aside , reserving the cooking water.
At this point, the broth with the meat will be ready . Cook the noodles in the same vegetable water, for the time indicated on the package . Drain the noodles and divide them among the plates, then pour the meat broth over them .
Arrange the vegetables around the plate , then add the pieces of meat . Your noodle soup is ready to be served !
It is recommended to consume the noodle soup immediately. You can store it in the refrigerator for up to one day.
Fresh noodles ( lamian ) are ideal for this recipe; alternatively, you can use dried noodles, available in well-stocked supermarkets or ethnic grocery stores.
