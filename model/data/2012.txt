name:
Tuna fillets on chickpea and red onion velouté
ingredients:
Tuna in oil: 9.2oz(260 g)- drained
Red onions: 5.3oz(150 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: 0.7tbsp(10 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Dill: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Precooked chickpeas: 1cup(240 g)
Garlic: 1clove
Shallot: 1
Rosemary: 1sprig
Extra virgin olive oil: ¾tsp(10 g)
Water: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4

To make the tuna fillets on chickpea and red onion velouté, first start with the chickpea cream: clean and slice the shallot . Heat 2 tsp of olive oil in a saucepan, add 1 peeled but whole clove of garlic and the sliced shallot .
Moisten with a little water , flavor with a sprig of rosemary and let the shallot wilt for about 5 minutes over moderate heat, stirring occasionally. Remove the clove of garlic
and the sprig of rosemary , salt, pepper, and pour in the chickpeas , add water until they are covered and continue cooking for about 20 minutes or until the chickpeas are very tender.
Meanwhile, clean the red onion and slice it , in a pan heat 2 tsp of olive oil and add the sliced onion , sauté over medium-high heat for about 5 minutes or until well golden and crunchy ; stir occasionally and salt at the end of cooking.
Meanwhile, the chickpeas will have finished cooking, blend them with an immersion blender to obtain a smooth and lump-free cream . Assemble your dish by spreading a layer of chickpea cream on the bottom, then a layer of onions toasted in the pan, and finally lay the drained tuna fillets on top, finish with pepper to taste and some dill leaves. Distribute the ingredients evenly on the 4 serving plates: the tuna fillets on chickpea and red onion velouté are ready to be served !
It is recommended to consume the tuna fillets on chickpea and red onion velouté immediately and freezing is not advised. Alternatively, it is possible to store only the chickpea cream separately in an airtight container for a couple of days. Freezing is not recommended.
If you use dried chickpeas, you need to halve the indicated amount, making sure to soak the legumes for at least 12 hours before boiling them.
