name:
Fig Cheesecake
ingredients:
Cookies: 5.3oz(150 g)
Walnut kernels: 1cup(110 g)
Butter: 4 ½tbsp(65 g)
Natural plain yogurt: 1 ½cup(350 g)
Fresh liquid cream: 0.8cup(200 g)- cold
Whole milk: 0.375cup(90 g)
Sugar: 5tbsp(60 g)
Wildflower honey: 1tbsp(20 g)
Gelatin in sheets: 2tsp(10 g)
Figs: 4
Walnut kernels: 1.3tbsp(10 g)

instructions:

To prepare the fig cheesecake, first melt the butter and let it cool. Put the cookies and walnuts in a food processor and blend until you get a crumbly mixture .
Transfer the mixture to a bowl and pour in the melted butter , then mix well to combine . Take an 8-inch springform pan, line it with parchment paper, and spread the cookie mixture on the bottom , pressing it down well with the back of a spoon.
Place the base in the fridge for at least 20 minutes to firm up . In the meantime, soak the gelatin in cold water. In a small saucepan, heat the milk with sugar and honey .
Add the well-squeezed gelatin and stir to dissolve completely . Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then pour it into a bowl with the yogurt .
Stir until you get a smooth cream, then set aside in the fridge. Now whip the cream with electric beaters .
Gently fold the whipped cream into the yogurt mixture with a spatula , stirring gently from bottom to top to avoid deflating it . Retrieve the pan from the fridge and pour the cream over the base .
Level the surface and let it set in the fridge for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight . Just before removing the cake from the pan, prepare the decoration: cut the figs in half and then into wedges .
Coarsely chop the walnuts . Once the cheesecake has set, carefully remove it from the pan and decorate the top with the walnut crumbs .
Finish with the fresh fig wedges and, if you like, a drizzle of honey to complete . Serve your homemade fig cheesecake immediately !
The fig cheesecake can be stored in the fridge, covered with a glass dome, for a maximum of 1-2 days, preferably without decoration.
Freezing is not recommended.
If you make one and a half times the recipe, you will get enough for a 9.5-inch diameter mold.
