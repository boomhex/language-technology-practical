name:
Baked Monkfish
ingredients:
Monkfish: 1.75lbs(800 g)- already cleaned
Lard: 7.1oz(200 g)
Marjoram: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: 1spoonful
Salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Asparagus: 1.1lbs(500 g)
Leeks: 1
Garlic: ½
Parsley: 12tbsp(35 g)
Pine nuts: 1tbsp(10 g)
Lemon juice: 2tbsp(30 g)
Fresh ginger: 2 ½tsp(5 g)
Sweet Paprika: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: 3spoonfuls

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:
To prepare baked monkfish, first clean it, remove the skin, and cut it lengthwise along the spine which will be discarded . Cut the flesh into chunks .
Salt and pepper . Lay down 6 strips of lardo , place the monkfish chunk on top, and roll it up. Repeat the same process for all pieces and set aside.
Remove the toughest part of the asparagus , then slice them into strips using a peeler .
Remove the greenest part of the leek and the roots , then cut the white part in half and then into julienne strips .
Peel the ginger and slice it into rounds . Squeeze the juice from half a lemon .
In a blender, place the pine nuts , a clove of garlic , and the ginger .
Add the sweet paprika , lemon juice , and adjust with salt and pepper .
Pour in the oil , add the parsley , and blend until smooth and homogeneous .
Heat a drizzle of oil in a pan, place the monkfish and cook until golden ; be careful not to break the lardo during cooking. Transfer the monkfish to a baking sheet covered with parchment paper and bake in a preheated fan oven at 355°F for 20 minutes .
Meanwhile, in a pan, pour a drizzle of oil, add half a clove of garlic with the core removed, leeks, and asparagus , season with salt and pepper . Sauté for 2 minutes over high heat .
After the cooking time, remove the monkfish from the oven and cut it into medallions . Place it on a plate and cover with leeks and asparagus .
Dress with parsley sauce and a few fresh marjoram leaves . Baked monkfish is ready to be served !
It is recommended to consume the baked monkfish immediately. If there are leftovers, you can store them in the fridge in an airtight container for 1-2 days; reheat in the oven at 300°F for 10-15 minutes before serving.
The parsley sauce can be prepared in advance and stored in the refrigerator for a day, in an airtight container.
For an extra touch of freshness, you can add a sprinkle of grated lemon zest.
