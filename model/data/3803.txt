The first thing you need to do when handling artichokes is to protect your hands with gloves to prevent them from turning black, or to rub them well with lemon juice; keep half a lemon handy, and occasionally rub your hands: it will help protect them! Then clean the artichokes (to see how to do it How to clean artichokes and place them one by one in a container with acidulated water (added with lemon juice) to prevent them from turning black. In a pan, crush 2 cloves of garlic, sauté them lightly in oil , add the drained artichoke wedges and stems and sauté them for a few moments.
Add some chopped parsley , keeping a handful aside (which you will use to sprinkle over the sautéed artichokes at the end of cooking); Also, prepare some vegetable broth, which you will use to moisten the artichokes whenever they dry out too much, then pepper and salt according to your taste. The cooking should continue for about 15-20 minutes depending on the size of the artichokes; you will know when it is time to turn off the heat by pricking a stem with a fork, which should be tender but not mushy. Serve the sautéed artichokes as soon as they are ready! .
