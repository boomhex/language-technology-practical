name:
Polenta Cake with Melted Heart
ingredients:
Corn and grain flour for instant polenta: 1cup(125 g)
Water: 2cups(500 g)
Coarse salt: ½tsp(3 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: 1.4tbsp(20 g)
Egg yolks: 4
Grana Padano PDO cheese: 3.5oz(100 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Fresh liquid cream: 10tsp(50 g)
White pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:

To prepare the polenta cakes with a melted heart, fill a high-sided pot with water and start heating. When the water boils, add salt , then pour in the corn and cereal flour gradually , stirring with a whisk (or wooden spoon) .
Cook on low heat for 4 minutes, continuing to stir constantly to avoid lumps. After the cooking time, the polenta will be ready; turn off the heat and set it aside. Take 4 aluminum molds with a capacity of 125 ml, fill each mold with about 1/4 cup of polenta .
Using a teaspoon, spread the polenta on the bottom and sides of each mold, leaving a central hollow . Here, insert 1 egg yolk into each mold , lightly salt .
and close the cake with a layer of polenta ; press the surface well to seal the cake as best as possible . Bake the polenta cakes in a preheated static oven at 350°F for 20 minutes. Once cooked, take them out of the oven .
Now prepare the accompanying sauce: pour the fresh liquid cream into a saucepan , heat it over moderate heat, then add the grated Grana Padano cheese , stir with the whisk to melt the cheese and obtain a homogeneous sauce.
Everything is ready to serve: unmold the cakes by turning them onto a serving plate , cover them with the Grana Padano sauce , and season with pepper to taste . Your polenta cakes with a melted heart are ready to be served!
The polenta cakes with a melted heart should be consumed immediately; storing in the refrigerator or freezing is not recommended.
Want to add an aromatic touch to the polenta? Once the polenta is ready, add some fresh thyme leaves!
