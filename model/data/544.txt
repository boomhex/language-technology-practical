name:
Christmas single serving
ingredients:
Butter: 7tbsp(100 g)- at room temperature
Brown sugar: ⅓cup(80 g)
Sugar: ¼cup(40 g)
Vanilla bean: 2
Dark chocolate 70%: 3.5oz(100 g)
Unsweetened cocoa powder: 2.67tbsp(20 g)
Baking soda: 4
Type 00 flour: 1cup(140 g)
Salt: 1.7tsp(10 g)
Lychees: ¾cup(200 g)- puree
Raspberries: ¼cup(50 g)- puree
Cornstarch: 2 ½tsp(10 g)
Rice starch: 1tsp(5 g)
Sugar: 2 ½tbsp(30 g)
White chocolate: 8.8oz(250 g)
Fresh liquid cream: 1cup(250 g)
Egg yolks: 3 ½tbsp(50 g)
Gelatin in sheets: 0.4oz(10 g)
Whole milk: 1.4tbsp(20 g)
Vanilla bean: 1

info:
Difficulty: Difficult
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 15 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average
Note + 3 hours of cooling

instructions:
To prepare the Christmas single serving, start with the crumble. Chop the dark chocolate . In a bowl, pour the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar . Mix the mixture with your hands .
When it is well combined , add the vanilla seeds and the chopped chocolate .
The cocoa powder and continue mixing with your hands . In a small bowl, dissolve the baking soda in a little water .
Add it to the combined mixture , also add the salt and the flour .
Knead quickly and crumble the obtained dough onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper , distributing it evenly . Let it rest in the refrigerator for 2 hours. Bake in a preheated static oven at 329°F for about 12 minutes.
Work on the gelée: in a bowl, mix the sugar with corn starch and rice flour . Pour the lychee puree and raspberry puree into a saucepan.
Bring to a boil , then pour in the powders and stir . Cook for 2 minutes and let cool.
Move on to the mousse: put the gelatin in cold water to soften it for about 10 minutes. Then pour it into 0.85 oz of warm milk and stir to dissolve it well .
In a bowl, pour the melted white chocolate, add the vanilla seeds , the egg yolks , and the gelatin dissolved in milk .
Stir to combine . In another bowl, pour the fresh cream and whip it with an electric whisk until semi-firm .
Incorporate the cream into the mousse , whip again with a hand whisk to obtain a fluffy mousse .
Retrieve the cooked crumble . Take 4 single serving glasses of 7.8 fl oz and pour a spoonful of crumble on the bottom , cover with a spoonful of mousse .
Make a second layer of crumble and mousse. Let it set in the freezer for an hour and then complete with the gelée . Serve your Christmas single serving .
We recommend consuming the Christmas single serving immediately. You can store it in the fridge for a couple of days.
The chocolate pieces will give extra crunchiness to the crumble.
