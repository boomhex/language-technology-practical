name:
Tagliatelle with squid ink with pecorino, mussels, and lard
ingredients:
Type 00 flour: 2cups(250 g)
Remilled durum wheat semolina: 1 ½cup(250 g)
Eggs: 5
Cuttlefish ink: 1 ½tbsp(20 g)
Mussels: 2.2lbs(1 kg)
Dry white wine: 3.4oz(100 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fresh liquid cream: ¾cup(200 g)
Pecorino Romano PDO cheese: 4.5oz(125 g)- (to grate)
Colonnata lard: 5.3oz(150 g)- sliced
Orange peel: 1- organic

instructions:

To prepare squid ink tagliolini with pecorino, mussels, and lard, start with the fresh pasta: crack the eggs into a bowl and add the squid ink , then whisk with a fork to combine . Pour this mixture into a bowl where you have mixed all-purpose flour and durum wheat semolina .
Incorporate the squid ink mixture, first working with a fork and then with your hands until you get a smooth and homogeneous dough . Wrap it in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes at room temperature.
After the resting time, divide the dough into two parts to make it easier to work with . Using a rolling pin or a pasta machine, roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/32 inch, then cut sheets about 8 inches long and dust with semolina .
Roll the sheets from the short side and cut the rolls into strips about 1/4 inch wide . Unroll the squid ink tagliatelle .
Place them on a tray dusted with semolina and store in the fridge until ready to use. Move on to the lard paste: place the lard slices in a mixer and blend on high speed for 30 seconds. Then add the grated orange zest .
Blend again for a minute until you have a homogeneous mixture . Transfer everything to a bowl, cover with plastic wrap in contact, and set aside at room temperature. Now focus on How to Clean and Open Mussels : scrape the shell with the back of a knife blade or a scrubber to remove all external impurities. Pull the beard from each mussel , then rinse thoroughly under running water. In a pot, heat a generous amount of oil, then add the mussels .
Deglaze with white wine and let all the alcohol evaporate. Cover the pot with a lid and cook for about 3 minutes, shaking the pot occasionally. When the mussels have opened, drain them .
Shell the mollusks and collect them in a bowl. Strain the mussels' cooking liquid and set aside .
Finally, prepare the pecorino cream: pour the cream into a saucepan and bring it almost to a boil, then remove the saucepan from the heat and add the grated Pecorino Romano . Stir vigorously with a whisk until completely melted. Pass the cream through a sieve to make it smoother and more homogeneous .
At this point, pour a ladle of the mussels' cooking liquid into a large pan , then add a spoonful of lard paste . Melt the lard over low heat, then add the mussels and cook for a couple of minutes.
Meanwhile, cook the tagliatelle in plenty of boiling salted water for about 2 minutes . Drain and transfer the tagliatelle to the pan with the sauce . Toss everything to combine, then turn off the heat and stir in another spoonful of lard paste .
If necessary, adjust the creaminess by adding a little more mussels' cooking water , then mix well . Plate the squid ink tagliatelle .
Add the shelled mussels and finish with the pecorino cream . The squid ink tagliatelle with pecorino, mussels, and lard are ready to be served !
It is recommended to consume the squid ink tagliatelle with pecorino, mussels, and lard as soon as they are ready, but it is possible to store the ingredients separately.
The lard paste can be stored in the refrigerator for a week in an airtight container.
The pecorino sauce can be stored in the refrigerator for 3 days in an airtight container.
The squid ink tagliolini can be stored on a floured tray in the refrigerator, covered with plastic wrap, for a day.
If you prefer, you can substitute the mussels with other seafood, such as clams or shrimp.
For a milder flavor, replace the pecorino with Parmigiano Reggiano PDO.
If you like it spicy, you can add fresh chili during the cooking of the mussels.
Moderately salt the pasta water as the elements of the recipe are already quite salty.
