name:
Tzatziki
ingredients:
Greek yogurt: 1 Â¾cup(400 g)
Cucumbers: 1
Garlic: 2cloves
Extra virgin olive oil: 1.4tbsp(20 g)
White wine vinegar: 2tsp(10 g)
Fine salt: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 10 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Very low
Note + 1 h to drain the cucumbers and 2-3 h of rest in the refrigerator

instructions:
To prepare the tzatziki sauce, first wash and remove the seeds from the cucumber, if present. Then grate the cucumber with a coarse grater, without removing the peel . Transfer the grated cucumber to a strainer placed over a bowl and let it rest for at least an hour, so that it loses its vegetation water .
After this time, peel the garlic cloves and remove the core with a knife , then finely chop them by crushing them with the blade of the knife until you obtain a sort of cream . Alternatively, you can use a mortar and pestle.
Pour the yogurt into a bowl, adjust with salt and add the oil and the vinegar , alternating between them, then mix well.
Now squeeze the cucumber pressing with the back of a spoon to eliminate all the vegetation water and add it to the yogurt .
Finally, add the garlic cream and stir again . Let your tzatziki sauce rest in the refrigerator for 2-3 hours to allow the flavors to blend together optimally !
To store the tzatziki sauce, you can cover the surface with oil and keep it in the refrigerator for no more than 2-3 days covered with plastic wrap.
Serve the tzatziki in a small bowl garnished with black olives, dill or mint, adding optionally another drizzle of oil on the surface!
Instead of vinegar, lemon juice can be used.
Try it with our chickpea nuggets , you'll find it delicious!
