To clean the squid, start by rinsing your mollusks thoroughly under a generous stream of cold water, then place them on your work surface to proceed with cleaning . Then take the head with two fingers and gently separate it from the mantle and set it aside. Inside the mantle, take the pen, which is the small transparent cartilage rod, and remove it .
Next, extract the squid's innards and rinse it. At this point, take the fish's head again. Hold it steady with two fingertips and, using a knife, cut around the eyes with a knife and by pressing the lower part of the eyes, remove them. Also, locate the beak found in the center of the tentacles and push it outward.
Using a knife, remove it . Finally, cut the mantle with a knife and peel the squid's skin . Repeat the operation for the tentacles as well. Rinse all parts of the fish under running water, and the squid will be ready for your preparations .
You can store the cleaned and eviscerated squid in the refrigerator for 1 day, placed on a plate and covered with clear plastic wrap, as described in the instructions.
You can also freeze them if you bought fresh and not previously frozen squid. Even with this preservation method, the squid must be properly cleaned and their internal viscera removed. Place them on a tray lined with parchment paper and put them in the freezer. Once frozen, transfer them to freezer bags: this way, you can store the squid for about 3 months. Before using them, remember to thaw them the night before by placing them in the refrigerator.
