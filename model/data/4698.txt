name:
Sole en papillote with steamed vegetables
ingredients:
Sole: 1.75lbs(800 g)- (4 fillets of 200 g each)
Lemons: 12slices
Parsley: to taste
Lemon thyme: to taste
Marjoram: to taste
Capers: to taste- in brine
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Egg yolks: 3
Clarified butter: ⅔cup(150 g)
White wine vinegar: 3spoonfuls
Water: to taste
Tarragon: to taste
Carrots: 1 ¾cup(200 g)
Cauliflower: 7cups(200 g)- (clean weight)
Broccoli: 1 ½cup(200 g)- (clean weight)

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 25 min
Cook time: 35 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:
Perfect for those looking for a tasty yet light idea, sole en papillote with steamed vegetables is a main course that combines the delicacy of fish with the freshness of seasonal vegetables. The en papillote cooking with lemon and aromatic herbs enhances the natural flavors of the sole, keeping its soft and buttery texture intact. The steamed vegetables, in this case, carrots, broccoli, and cauliflower, are colorful and crunchy, completing the dish to make it balanced and irresistible. But what truly makes it special is the homemade Hollandaise sauce with tarragon, adding a delicious and enveloping touch. Whether it's a family dinner or a quick yet refined lunch, the recipe for sole en papillote with steamed vegetables is an excellent choice! To prepare the sole en papillote with steamed vegetables, start with the vegetables. Separate the cauliflower florets and cut the larger ones in half . Do the same with the broccoli .
Move on to the carrots: peel them and cut them into diagonal slices . Now heat some water in a steamer and add a pinch of salt. Place the steaming basket above. Once it reaches a boil, place the carrots inside the basket . Cover with a lid and cook for 5 minutes.
After this time, add broccoli and cauliflower , cover again with the lid , and cook for another 10 minutes. The vegetables should be tender yet still crunchy . Season with salt.
Focus on the Hollandaise sauce. Pour the vinegar into a small pan and a tablespoon of water. Bring to heat and cook until the mixture is reduced by half; this will take a few minutes . Meanwhile, melt the clarified butter. At this point, place the yolks in a heatproof bowl in a bain-marie .
Begin to whisk them with a hand whisk , then add the reduced vinegar mixture . Continue whisking until the mixture thickens (it should reach about 149°F), then slowly add the melted clarified butter while continuing to whisk . Stir until you obtain a creamy sauce.
Off the heat, season with chopped tarragon , a pinch of salt, and pepper . Your Hollandaise sauce is ready .
Now proceed to cook the fish. Cut the lemons into slices , then place 3 slices on a sheet of parchment paper and arrange the aromatic herbs on top .
Lay a fish fillet on top and season with salt , pepper, and oil .
Add a handful of capers on top . Now wrap the sole fillet first in parchment paper and then in foil to seal it well and form a packet.
Proceed in the same way for all 4 fillets, placing them one by one on a baking sheet . Bake in a preheated static oven at 350°F for 15-20 minutes. The cooking time may vary depending on the size of the fillets. Remove the sole en papillote from the oven and carefully unwrap them , then plate. Place the vegetables on the bottom of the plate (you can reheat them for a few minutes in a bain-marie) .
Lay the fish on top and garnish with the tarragon Hollandaise sauce . Your sole en papillote with steamed vegetables is ready to be served !
The sole en papillote with steamed vegetables can be stored in the refrigerator for one day in an airtight container.
It is preferable to prepare the Hollandaise sauce at the moment.
If the Hollandaise sauce is too thick, you can dilute it with a bit of water or vinegar. Also, try it in the Plaice with Hollandaise sauce recipe!
You can vary the vegetables according to your season.
