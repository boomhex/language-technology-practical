name:
Rabbit Ragù
ingredients:
Rabbit: - 1 whole approximately 1 kg
Canned tomatoes: 1 ¾cup(400 g)
Vegetable broth: to taste
Red wine: 2glasses
Red wine: to taste- to marinate the rabbit
Carrots: 1
Celery: 1- rib
Red onions: 1- large
Garlic: 3cloves
Bay leaves: 3leaves
Sage: 5leaves
Rosemary: 1sprig
Juniper berries: 3
Extra virgin olive oil: 5spoonfuls
Fine salt: to taste
Ground black pepper: 6

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 20 min
Cook time: 60 min
Serving: 6
Cost: Low

instructions:

Remove the head, innards, and any trace of fat from the rabbit, then wash it under cold water and cut it into pieces . Place the rabbit pieces in a container along with juniper berries, black peppercorns, two cloves of garlic, half an onion, a sprig of rosemary, bay leaves, and sage leaves , then cover everything with red wine and let it marinate for at least two hours in a cool place (or overnight in the refrigerator).
After the necessary time has passed, drain the rabbit and discard the marinade wine. Prepare a mince of onion, garlic, carrot, and celery and let it brown in the oil , then add the rabbit pieces to the pan and let them brown over medium heat along with the aromas.
Add a glass of red wine and let it evaporate, then salt, add another glass of wine, and cover the rabbit with a lid, letting it cook over low heat until it starts to fall apart, adding hot broth as needed until the end of cooking. Let the rabbit cool slightly, then, using your hands, debone all the meat (much easier when the rabbit is cooked!), chop it with a knife and add it to its cooking base in the pan .
Add the tomato pulp and cook for another 10 minutes, adjusting salt and pepper . Boil the chosen pasta and season it with the rabbit ragù.
The rabbit ragù recipe described above is very simple, almost basic, but if you want to give it more flavor or enrich it, you can also add mushrooms, sausage, or pancetta cubes.
