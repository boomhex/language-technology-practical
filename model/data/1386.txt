name:
Elderflower Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting
ingredients:
Elderberry syrup: 11.3tbsp(170 g)
Eggs: 8.3oz(235 g)- (about 4)
Sugar: ⅓cup(70 g)
Whole milk: 1tbsp(20 g)- at room temperature
Butter: 4tbsp(60 g)- melted
Type 00 flour: 1 ⅓cup(165 g)
Baking powder: 4tsp(16 g)
Elderberry syrup: 6 ½tbsp(90 g)
Cream cheese: 0.83cup(200 g)
Powdered sugar: ½cup(60 g)
Butter: 4tbsp(60 g)- plastic
Mint: to taste
Lavender flowers: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 8
Note + 30 minutes of frosting resting time

instructions:

To make the elderflower cake with cream cheese frosting, pour the eggs and sugar into a bowl , whisk the mixture with electric beaters until foamy, and then gradually pour in the elderflower syrup .
Continue beating with the beaters and also incorporate the melted butter at room temperature and the milk . Keep beating with the beaters until you obtain a homogeneous mixture .
At this point, add the sifted dry ingredients: first the flour and then the baking powder . Mix with a spatula and then pour the batter into a greased 9-inch round pan .
The batter is ready for baking : bake in a static oven, preheated to 350°F for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the frosting: in a bowl, pour the cream cheese and softened butter (just take it out of the fridge fifteen minutes before use) , beat with electric beaters, and pour in the elderflower syrup .
Once the syrup is absorbed, add the powdered sugar . You will obtain a thick cream , cover it with plastic wrap , and let it rest in the fridge for at least thirty minutes.
Meanwhile, the cake will have finished baking; take it out of the oven and let it cool. Once the cake is cold, remove it from the pan, level it by cutting off the excess dome with a serrated knife .
Spread the frosting with the back of a spoon , you should cover the entire surface . Decorate with mint leaves and lavender flowers and let it rest in the fridge for at least twenty minutes before serving the elderflower cake with cream cheese frosting.
Store the elderflower cake with cream cheese frosting in the fridge and consume within 3 days. You can freeze the baked base.
Have fun decorating the cake with fresh fruit to your liking: strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, or whatever the season offers!
