name:
Pasta Soup with Chickpeas and Broccoli
ingredients:
Mini Pipe Rigate pasta: 3cups(300 g)
Cluster tomatoes: 5.3oz(150 g)- (1 medium)
Fresh scallion: 2.5oz(70 g)
Precooked chickpeas: 1.7cups(400 g)
Broccoli: 0.9lb(400 g)
Bay leaves: 1leaf
Water: 4 ¼cups(1 l)
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 20 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:

To prepare the pasta soup with chickpeas and broccoli, start by slicing the white part of the spring onion , then wash and dice the tomato into medium-sized pieces . Remove the broccoli florets and rinse them.
In a large pot, pour a drizzle of oil and gently sauté the spring onion over low heat, stirring often with a wooden spoon . After about 1 minute, you can add the diced tomato and the bay leaf (fresh or dried) ,
the broccoli florets and 7 oz of pre-cooked chickpeas, drained from their preserving water . Mix everything and cook over medium heat: after a few minutes, add some water, setting aside just 3.5 oz of the total amount . Salt to taste and cook the soup over medium heat for about 10 minutes.
In a separate bowl, pour the remaining 7 oz of chickpeas and add the 3.5 oz of water set aside , then blend everything with an immersion blender . After the 10 minutes of cooking the soup, add the chickpea puree thus obtained .
Cook everything for another 5-8 minutes, taking care to remove the bay leaf, before adding the pasta, cooking it for 5 minutes . Stir occasionally and, once ready, serve your soup with another drizzle of oil for each plate . The pasta soup with chickpeas and broccoli is ready to be enjoyed piping hot !
Store the pasta soup with chickpeas and broccoli in the fridge for no more than 1-2 days. If during cooking, the water from the soup evaporates too much, be sure to add the necessary water to replenish the liquids.
You can add turmeric powder (about 1 tsp) to this pasta soup with chickpeas and broccoli and flavor it with your favorite herbs: marjoram, thyme, parsley, sage, rosemary. If there is leftover pasta and you prefer not to reheat it, you can make delicious pasta mini-flans in cocotte by adding béchamel to the leftover pasta and gratinating with Parmesan, grated Emmental, or fontina.
