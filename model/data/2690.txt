name:
Pumpkin, Yogurt and Salted Caramel Loaf Cake
ingredients:
Mantuan pumpkin: 12.3oz(350 g)- cleaned
Greek yogurt 0% fat: ¾cup(200 g)
Type 00 flour: 2 ¼cups(280 g)
Nut flour: 0.83cup(80 g)
Vegetable oil: 0.8cup(200 ml)
Sugar: ¾cup(150 g)
Eggs: 3- (medium)
Baking powder: 3.3tsp(16 g)
Fine salt: to taste
Sugar: ½cup(100 g)
Fresh liquid cream: 3 ½tbsp(50 ml)- hot
Fine salt: 1tsp
Butter: 5.3tbsp(75 g)- (cold)

info:
Difficulty: Average
Prep time: 30 min
Cook time: 1 h 20 min
Serving: 8
Cost: Low

instructions:

To make the pumpkin, yogurt, and salted caramel loaf cake, cut the pumpkin into wedges, remove the skin and seeds, and blend the slices in a mixer to obtain a pulp . In the bowl of a stand mixer, pour the eggs and sugar with a pinch of salt
and whisk them , then pour the mixture into a bowl and slowly add the vegetable oil . Once you've fully incorporated the oil, add the sifted flour , mix
also add the hazelnut flour and the baking powder. Then incorporate the blended pumpkin , the yogurt , and mix the batter with a spoon.
Line a loaf pan of dimensions 11.8x4.3 inches with parchment paper, pour the batter into the pan and bake in a preheated static oven at 356°F for 70 minutes (or in a convection oven at 320°F for 60 minutes). Meanwhile, prepare the salted caramel: place a pan over the heat and gradually add the sugar and let it melt and turn brown over low heat. Do not stir the sugar at this stage but only rotate the pan. Meanwhile, heat the cream, and when the caramel is smooth ,
incorporate the hot cream . Mix the caramel with a whisk and add the salt and the cold butter cut into small cubes .
Continue stirring until you get a smooth and homogeneous mixture, then emulsify it with an immersion blender . At this point, the syrup is ready . Transfer it to a wide, shallow bowl and cover with plastic wrap , then place it in the refrigerator to set.
Once baking is finished, remove the loaf cake from the oven, let it cool, and then unmold it onto a wire rack . Transfer the caramel into a piping bag with a plain nozzle and garnish the surface of the loaf cake . The pumpkin, yogurt, and salted caramel loaf cake is ready to be enjoyed!
Store the pumpkin, yogurt, and salted caramel loaf cake under a glass dome for 2-3 days. It is possible to freeze the loaf cake after baking, perhaps portioned so that only the necessary amount can be thawed.
To add a crunchy note, you can garnish the loaf cake with hazelnut crumbs to be distributed over the caramel.
