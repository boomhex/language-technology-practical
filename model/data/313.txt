name:
Seafood Pasta and Potatoes
ingredients:
Mixed Pasta: 11.3oz(320 g)
Smoked scamorza cheese: 10.5oz(300 g)
Parmigiano Reggiano PDO cheese: 1.8oz(50 g)- (to grate)
Green Peperoncino: to taste
Parsley: to taste
Basil: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Potatoes: 0.9lb(400 g)
Cherry tomatoes: 1cup(200 g)
Onions: ⅓cup(50 g)
Carrots: 1.75oz(50 g)
Celery: ½cup(50 g)
Vegetable broth: 2.1cups(500 g)
Bay leaves: 1leaf
Mussels: 2.2lbs(1 kg)
Garlic: 1clove
Fresh chili pepper: 1
White wine: 0.67cup(150 g)
Water: 0.67cup(150 g)- cold
Parsley: 1sprig
Calamari (squid): 12.5oz(350 g)- (cleaned)

instructions:
Seafood pasta with potatoes has its roots deep in Campania, especially around Naples. Folks here love mixing simple stuff—like potatoes and short pasta—with those rich flavors you get from fresh seafood. Really, it's a thing. This updated version, inspired by chef Sabrina Macrì, turns the usual ‘pasta e patate’ into something kinda fancy with a good handful of mussels and squid. The trick? It’s the ‘risottata’ way of cooking, where you add broth slowly, so the pasta really soaks up all the tender flavors and everything turns out super creamy. You toss in a little smoked provola cheese—it melts right in—bringing a hint of that woodsy taste. And Parmigiano? Sprinkled on top for a golden, salty finish. Perfect for anyone wanting a classic seafood pasta dish with a modern twist. No question.
Plus, you’ll notice how the potatoes give the sauce a velvety texture that clings to every bite. It makes each mouthful a bit of a surprise. In Campania, people are known for tweaking their seafood pasta dishes based on what’s fresh. Sometimes they’ll throw in clams or even shrimp, but trust me, the combo of potatoes, mussels and squid really stands out. This version brings land and sea together in a way that shows off just how creative Neapolitan cooks are. The smoked provola isn’t found everywhere—which is awesome—so it makes the dish feel special. It melts into the sauce and adds depth without overpowering the delicate seafood. Honestly, it's great for a long Sunday lunch or a chill weeknight seafood pasta dinner . Pretty simple, really.
Each bite has a bit of the tangy sea and the sweet comfort of potatoes, balanced out by the salty hit of Parmigiano. Even with these fancy touches, this seafood pasta with potatoes stays true to its roots, showing how traditional flavors can mix with new ideas for something really satisfying. It’s a dish where every part counts, and the moist, flavorful sauce is what brings it all together. For real. Enjoy this taste of Campania and savor the unique blend of textures and tastes that make this dish truly special.

To prepare the seafood pasta and potatoes, first make the Vegetable broth and keep it warm. Start with the How to Clean and Open Mussels : remove the beard, scrape the shells well, and rinse them under running water . Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan with a clove of garlic, a parsley stalk, and a piece of chili pepper , then add the mussels .
Deglaze with white wine , then pour in cold water to create a more abundant base. Let the mussels open over high heat with the lid on, it will take about 5 minutes .
When they are all open, remove them from the heat and strain the cooking liquid , which you will set aside. Separate the mollusks from the shells , leaving some whole to decorate the dish at the end.
Cover the mussels with their water and set them aside . Move on to the potatoes: peel them and cut them into regular cubes , then place them in a bowl with cold water to prevent them from turning brown .
Finally, focus on cleaning the squid: remove the innards, skin, and beak, then rinse them well and cut the bodies into rings and the tentacles into pieces .
Prepare the sauté: finely chop onion, celery, and carrot , and halve the cherry tomatoes . Heat an abundant drizzle of oil in a large saucepan, then add the onion .
Also add the chopped celery and carrot . Salt immediately and flavor with a bay leaf , then let it sauté for a few minutes. When the vegetables are soft, add the cherry tomatoes .
Cook over high heat for about 5 minutes, to slightly wilt them . At this point, add the drained potatoes and mix them well with the sauté to flavor them. Add the hot vegetable broth .
Salt and pepper , then let it cook for 10 minutes, until the potatoes start to soften. Meanwhile, in a separate pan, heat a drizzle of oil with a clove of garlic and add the squid, sautéing them over high heat . Deglaze with white wine and let it evaporate.
When a nice crust has formed, salt and pepper . Now add the pasta directly to the saucepan and cook for 10 minutes over low heat, stirring often, as you would with a risotto. Halfway through cooking, add the filtered mussel water .
Also add the squid , mix and pour more water if needed. When there are 2 minutes left before the end of cooking, add the shelled mussels and gently stir, letting it simmer to thoroughly blend all the flavors .
Meanwhile, cut the smoked scamorza into cubes . When the pasta is ready, remove it from the heat, season with chili powder and plenty of chopped parsley .
Add the scamorza and a sprinkle of grated Parmigiano Reggiano , then mix well until the cheeses melt and the texture becomes thick and velvety.
Plate the seafood pasta and potatoes , finishing each portion with a few whole mussels for decoration and a fresh basil leaf to scent the dish .
We recommend consuming the seafood pasta and potatoes immediately.
As an alternative to mixed pasta, you can use other shapes like tubetti or ditalini, which are well-suited for creamy pasta preparations.
You can replace the vegetable broth with water.
