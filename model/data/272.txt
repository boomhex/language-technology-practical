name:
Fennel en Papillote
ingredients:
Fennel: 6cups(600 g)
Taggiasca olives: 3.5oz(100 g)- pitted
Sun-dried tomatoes in oil: 2.8oz(80 g)
Capers in vinegar: 2tbsp(30 g)
Dried chili pepper: to taste
Fine salt: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 30 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Low

instructions:
To prepare fennel en papillote, first wash the fennel and remove the ends , then cut them in half and slice thinly . Keep the fennel fronds. Drain the sun-dried tomatoes from the preserving oil and chop roughly .
In a bowl, combine the fennel, sun-dried tomatoes , olives , and capers .
Add the reserved fennel fronds , the crushed dried chili pepper , and the salt .
Mix to season . Prepare the papillotes: cut 4 sheets of parchment paper, then place a quarter of the seasoned fennel in the center of each . Finally, fold the sheet towards the center to enclose the filling .
Twist the ends like a candy and place the 4 parcels on a baking sheet . Bake in a preheated static oven at 400Â°F for 30 minutes. After this time, remove the fennel en papillote from the oven and garnish with extra fresh fennel fronds as desired!
Fennel en papillote can be stored in the refrigerator for 2-3 days.
You can freeze the fennel after cooking.
In summer, you can use fresh cherry tomatoes instead of sun-dried tomatoes.
You can use your flavorful fennel en papillote as a side dish or even as a topping for pasta or bruschetta!
