name:
Tagliatelle with peas and ricotta
ingredients:
Egg Tagliatelle pasta: 10.5oz(300 g)
Peas: 1 ¼cup(200 g)
Ricotta di bufala cheese: 3.5oz(100 g)
Sun-dried tomatoes in oil: 1.5oz(40 g)
Vegetable broth: ¾cup(200 g)
Garlic: 1clove
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Thyme: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 25 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

To prepare the tagliatelle with peas and ricotta, first start by making the Vegetable broth . Once you've strained it, keep it hot. Then place a drizzle of oil, a slightly crushed clove of garlic in its skin , and let it brown in a pan. Then add the peas , sauté them for a minute, then remove the garlic clove .
Pour 5 fl oz of hot broth and let it cook on low heat for about 20 minutes until the peas are tender . Meanwhile, drain the sun-dried tomatoes and slice them into strips .
Crumble the ricotta into a small bowl and place both in the refrigerator. After 20 minutes, put a pot full of salted water on the stove and bring it to a boil, it will be used for cooking the pasta. In the meantime, transfer the cooked peas into a blender and add some broth .
Blend everything until you get a smooth and soft cream . If it's too thick, add a little more broth; then adjust the salt .
As soon as the water starts to boil, cook the tagliatelle . Meanwhile, transfer the pea cream into another pan and once the pasta is cooked, drain it (keeping some of the cooking water aside) and transfer it to the pan .
Sauté the tagliatelle, adding cooking water as needed , the sun-dried tomatoes , thyme leaves , and freshly ground pepper to taste.
At this point transfer the tagliatelle to serving plates , finish each plate with crumbled ricotta, and serve your tagliatelle with peas and ricotta while still hot !
It is recommended to consume the tagliatelle with peas and ricotta immediately. Alternatively, they can be stored for a maximum of one day in the refrigerator, in an airtight container. Freezing is not recommended.
Instead of peas, you can use another seasonal vegetable, such as asparagus, fava beans, or zucchini.
If you prefer, you can replace the sun-dried tomatoes with Cherry Tomatoes Confit .
