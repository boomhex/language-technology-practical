name:
Tuna fillet with pistachio pesto
ingredients:
Tuna filet: 8.1oz(230 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Unsalted pistachios: 1oz(30 g)- not roasted
Extra virgin olive oil: 2tbsp(30 g)
Potatoes: 0.7lb(300 g)
Garlic: 2cloves
Extra virgin olive oil: 1.3tbsp(20 g)
Smoked paprika: ¼tsp

info:
Difficulty: Very easy
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 35 min
Serving: 2

instructions:

To prepare the tuna fillet with pistachio pesto, first preheat the oven to 392°F in static mode, then wash and peel the potatoes with a peeler. Cut the potatoes in half lengthwise , then slice them lengthwise and crosswise to obtain cubes about 0.4 inches in size .
Transfer the cubed potatoes into a bowl, add the oil , salt, paprika , garlic cloves , and mix thoroughly.
Pour the seasoned potatoes into a baking tray and bake in the preheated static oven at 392°F for about 30 minutes. Meanwhile, proceed with preparing the pistachio pesto: place the shelled pistachios in a food processor , add the oil and a pinch of salt,
and blend until you obtain a smooth cream . Then, transfer the pistachio pesto into a bowl and set aside. When the potatoes are golden brown, remove them from the oven and proceed with cooking the tuna: heat a drizzle of oil in a non-stick pan, then add the tuna fillets ,
add salt and pepper, and cook over medium-high heat for 2-3 minutes. At this point, gently turn the tuna fillets with a spatula and cook the other side for 1-2 minutes , then remove them from the pan.
Place a spoonful of pistachio pesto on each plate , lay the tuna fillet on top , and add the golden potatoes. Your tuna fillet with pistachio pesto is ready to be served !
It is recommended to serve the tuna fillet with pistachio pesto immediately. The pistachio pesto can be stored in a jar in the refrigerator for 4-5 days or can be frozen. It is not recommended to freeze the complete dish.
You can try other tasty variations of this dish by accompanying the tuna fillet with a classic Genoese pesto (Pesto alla genovese) or a Marjoram pesto , which has a stronger and slightly bitter flavor.
