name:
Homemade Apple Juice
ingredients:
Gala apples: 14.1oz(400 g)- (to clean)
Sugar: 0.4cup(80 g)
Lemon juice: 2 ¾tbsp(40 g)
Water: 2.1cups(500 g)

instructions:
Making homemade apple juice is one of those things that just feels extra fresh when you whip it up yourself. Seriously good. In Italy, folks really love using crisp Gala apples—because they’re both juicy and kinda sweet—which makes the whole homemade apple juice thing way, way better than anything store-bought. Plus, no weird stuff added, just real fruit, and nothing else—so you get that super pure, natural apple juice flavor every time. People in different regions mix up the apples they use. I mean, some like a tart bite, so they’ll toss in a few Granny Smiths, while others want a more mellow vibe with Golden Delicious. Either way, the point is to have fun. Play around until you hit the perfect mix for you.
One cool thing about making this at home—pretty simple, really—is how easy it is. No need for fancy machines or crazy steps. It keeps the whole apple juice recipe focused on those tender apples. And here's the thing, a glass of fresh apple juice goes with just about anything. For real. Some families pour it out with breakfast, others pack it in lunchboxes for a healthy snack. And you know what? Some folks even use it as a base for nonalcoholic cocktails or to give desserts a little tangy apple kick.
In Italian kitchens, you’ll see people adding this juice to make cakes extra moist or whipping up fruity sauces. You can even take it further with other drinks—like a lemon-mint juice—just to keep things interesting and stick with that healthy, homemade vibe. Making your own DIY apple juice isn’t just about taste. It's also about knowing exactly what goes in, so no surprise sugars or fake flavors sneak in. The taste is golden and feels way more real than bottled stuff. Plus, it’s kinda satisfying to know you can whip up something this tasty from just a few apples. Every batch lets you try new combos and share a bit of that Italian love for good, honest food with everyone at the table. Pretty much. So, so good.

To prepare homemade apple juice, wash the Gala apples very well, peel them, remove the core , and cut them into chunks .
Gather the apples in a bowl , add the lemon juice , and set aside .
In a high-sided saucepan, pour the water and sugar , bring to a boil for 3 minutes, stirring with a spoon . Add the chopped apples and continue to cook for 15 minutes over medium heat.
After the cooking time of the apples, blend everything with an immersion blender to obtain a smooth juice. Transfer the juice into clean bottles and store it in the refrigerator to serve chilled .
Homemade apple juice can be stored in the refrigerator for 3 days.
