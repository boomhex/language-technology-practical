name:
Couscous with Zucchini and Shrimp
ingredients:
Couscous: 1 ¾cup(300 g)
Shrimps: 0.9lb(400 g)- to clean
Zucchini: 1cup(200 g)
Garlic: 1clove
Saffron: 0.1tsp(0.3 g)
Parsley: to taste
Lemon peel: to taste
Extra virgin olive oil: to taste
Fine salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Carrots: ¼cup(50 g)
Celery: ½cup(50 g)
Onions: 0.3cup(50 g)
Water: 2.1cups(1 l)
Coarse salt: to taste

info:
Difficulty: Easy
Prep time: 45 min
Cook time: 35 min
Serving: 4
Cost: Average

instructions:
To prepare couscous with zucchini and shrimp, first clean the shrimp: remove the head , then remove the shell and the intestine .
Set aside the scraps and cut the flesh into chunks . In a pot, heat a drizzle of oil and add the coarsely chopped carrot, celery, and onion . Add the shrimp scraps as well .
Add the salt and water . Bring to a boil and cook for about 20-30 minutes, occasionally skimming off the foam that forms on the surface .
After the cooking time, strain the stock and measure out 1 1/4 cups, which you will need for the couscous . Now dissolve the saffron in a little warm water .
Place the couscous in a large bowl, then add the dissolved saffron , the salt , and the 1 1/4 cups of hot stock .
Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest for 5 minutes. In the meantime, cut the zucchini into cubes . After the couscous resting time, fluff it with a fork .
In a pan, flavor a drizzle of oil with a clove of garlic, then add the zucchini , salt and pepper. Cook over medium heat for 15 minutes, then add the shrimp . Continue cooking for another 5 minutes, then remove the garlic .
At this point, add the zucchini and shrimp to the bowl with the couscous , then season with chopped parsley and a drizzle of oil .
Flavor with grated lemon zest and mix to combine everything . Your couscous with zucchini and shrimp is ready to be enjoyed !
The couscous with zucchini and shrimp can be stored in the refrigerator for up to one day.
The shellfish stock can be stored in the refrigerator for 2 days or can be frozen.
If you prefer, you can replace the parsley with mint for a more intense aromatic touch!
