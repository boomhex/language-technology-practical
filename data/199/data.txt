name:
Savory cheesecake with pesto and cherry tomatoes
ingredients:
Taralli (Round breadsticks): 8.8oz(250 g)
Butter: ½cup(125 g)
Cow's milk ricotta cheese: 1.4cups(350 g)
Cream cheese: 7oz(200 g)
Grana Padano PDO cheese: 1oz(30 g)- (to grate)
Milk: ½cup(100 g)
Gelatin in sheets: 0.3oz(8 g)
Salt: to taste
Black pepper: to taste
Cherry tomatoes: 1.67cups(250 g)
Genoese pesto (Basil sauce): ½cup(120 g)
Garlic: 2cloves
Sugar: 1tbsp(15 g)
Extra virgin olive oil: 1 ½tbsp(20 g)

This savory cheesecake is one of those dishes that’s just a delightful surprise. Really, for anyone trying it for the first time, it’s a treat. And, it's kinda like a cross between a savory tart and a cheesecake, giving you that familiar richness with a tangy kick from the pesto. The cherry tomatoes? They’re typically confited, becoming juicy and soft, perfectly balancing the salty, herby pesto. Liguria is renowned for maximizing the flavors of basil and tomatoes, and this pesto cheesecake showcases these ingredients beautifully.
So, while cheesecake is usually a dessert, this cherry tomato cheesecake makes for a fantastic appetizer at a summer gathering or a light meal paired with a salad. And some folks like to mix things up with sun-dried tomatoes or a touch of ricotta for extra moist texture. But the crunchy base, creamy filling, and tender tomatoes? They really really bring out the best of northern Italian flavors. To prepare the savory cheesecake with pesto and cherry tomatoes, first focus on preparing the confit cherry tomatoes. Wash the cherry tomatoes and dry them with paper towels or a clean dish towel, then cut them in half 1 . In a pan, pour the oil 2 and add the garlic 3 .
Place the cherry tomatoes in the pan 4 , add the sugar 5 , distributing it evenly over all the cherry tomatoes, and adjust the salt 6 .
Also season with pepper 7 and oregano 8 . Cook on high flame for about 8 minutes, or until the sugar has caramelized 9 . Set aside the confit cherry tomatoes.
To prepare the base, melt the butter 10 , either in the microwave or in a pan on low flame. Put the taralli in a food processor 11 and blend them to a powder 12 .
Transfer the blended taralli into a bowl and pour in the melted butter, then mix 10 until you get a sandy consistency. Pour the mixture into an 8-inch diameter mold 20 , previously buttered and lined with parchment paper. Level with the back of a spoon 15 . Place the mold in the refrigerator to firm up the base.
Soak the gelatin in cold water 16 . In the meantime, prepare the cheese cream: in a bowl, combine the ricotta, cream cheese 17 , and grated Grana Padano DOP 18 .
Adjust the salt 19 and pepper 20 . Mix until you get a homogeneous mixture 21 .
Pour the milk into a saucepan and heat it until almost boiling, remove from heat and add the well-squeezed gelatin 22 . Stir to dissolve it completely 23 . Pour the milk into the bowl with the cheeses 24 .
Mix 25 until you get a fairly liquid and homogeneous cream. Take the mold out of the refrigerator and pour the cream onto the base 26 , tilt the mold to level the cream 27 and place it back in the refrigerator for an hour.
After the resting time, remove the cheesecake from the mold and spread the pesto on the surface 28 . Garnish with the confit cherry tomatoes 29 and a few basil leaves 30 . The savory cheesecake with pesto and cherry tomatoes is ready to be enjoyed!
The savory cheesecake with pesto and cherry tomatoes can be stored in the refrigerator for 2 days.
Instead of basil leaves, you can use arugula.
For a perfect cheesecake presentation, add the basil and confit cherry tomatoes just before serving!
