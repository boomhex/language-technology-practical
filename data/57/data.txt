name:
Gluten-free cake
text:
Rice flour: 1 ⅓cup(170 g)
Almond flour: ⅓cup(30 g)
Gluten free  potato starch: 6tbsp(45 g)
Eggs: 7.8oz(220 g)- (about 4)
Sugar: 0.9cup(180 g)
Sunflower seed oil: 10 ½tbsp(150 g)
Gluten free powdered yeast for sweets: 3 ¾tsp(16 g)
Lemon peel: 1
Vanilla extract: to taste
Fine salt: to taste

To prepare the gluten-free cake, pour the eggs and sugar into a large bowl 1 . Using electric beaters, beat the eggs with the sugar 2 until you get a well-whipped mixture. When it is light and frothy, add the seed oil slowly, continuing to work with the electric beaters 3 .
Add the vanilla extract 4 , grated lemon zest 5 , and a pinch of salt 6 .
Work again for a few seconds 7 . Sift the rice flour 8 and baking powder 9 into the batter.
Mix with a hand whisk from the bottom up with slow and precise movements 10 . Also, add the sifted potato starch 11 and almond flour 12 .
Mix with a hand whisk 13 to fully incorporate the powders, until you obtain a smooth and homogeneous batter 14 . Transfer the mixture to an 8-inch diameter pan lined with parchment paper or greased and floured with rice flour 15 .
At this point, bake in a static oven at 350°F for 45-50 minutes 16 . Always do the toothpick test before removing the cake from the oven 17 . Let it cool slightly, then remove your gluten-free cake and sprinkle with powdered sugar 18 !
The gluten-free cake can be stored under a glass dome for 2-3 days.
Alternatively, you can freeze it, perhaps already cut into slices.
If you prefer, you can substitute almond flour with the same amount of hazelnut or pistachio flour.
